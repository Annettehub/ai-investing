**Q: Why did you choose to build your own infrastructure stack rather than using existing services, and how does that serve as a competitive moat?**
> **Q: 你为什么选择构建自己的基础设施堆栈而不是使用现有服务，这如何成为竞争护城河？**

A: We operate under the philosophy that difficulty is very defensible. From day one, we chose to do the hard things, like maintaining our own ledger in 25 countries and becoming principal members with networks like MasterCard so we can issue cards directly without a bank in between. Our core IP isn't the front-facing product; it's the central layer we built to connect to 50 or 60 different partners and abstract that complexity. By owning the infrastructure, we can issue statements in 25 countries in under 24 hours and maintain a consistent user experience regardless of the region. This level of control also significantly expands our margins—moving from 40% two years ago to over 80% today—because we capture the lion's share of the value instead of paying it out to middleman partners.
> A: 我们遵循 “困难是非常可防御的” 的理念。从第一天起，我们选择做艰难的事情，比如在 25 个国家维护自己的账本，并成为像万事达卡这样的网络的主要成员，以便我们可以直接发行卡，而不需要中间银行。我们的核心知识产权不是面向客户的产品，而是我们构建的中央层，以便连接 50 或 60 个不同的合作伙伴，并抽象出这种复杂性。通过拥有基础设施，我们可以在 24 小时内在 25 个国家发行对账单，并保持一致的用户体验，无论地区如何。这种控制水平也显著扩大了我们的利润——从两年前的 40% 增加到今天的 80% 以上——因为我们捕获了价值的最大份额，而不是将其支付给中介伙伴。

**Q: How has the integration of stablecoins fundamentally changed your payment rails and operational efficiency?**
> **Q: 稳定币的整合如何从根本上改变了你的支付轨道和运营效率？**

A: In Latin America, stablecoins are a lived experience, not a theory, and they have become the infrastructure that powers our growth. Previously, a cross-border payment between Mexico and the U.S. required a slow handoff between multiple correspondent banks. Now, we collect local currency on the ground in Mexico, instantly release USDC to settle the transaction, and pay out on the U.S. side. This allows us to settle in one hour instead of one or two days. We don't even pitch "stablecoin" to our enterprise CFOs; we pitch "Jeeves Instant Pay." They care about trust, reliability, and cost, and because we have built our own ledger that handles both fiat and stablecoin, we can offer them a superior, faster, and more cost-effective product without them needing to understand the underlying blockchain technology.
> A: 在拉丁美洲，稳定币是一种现实体验，而不是理论，它们已成为推动我们增长的基础设施。之前，从墨西哥到美国的跨境支付需要多个代理银行之间缓慢的交接。现在，我们在墨西哥收集当地货币，立即释放 USDC 来结算交易，并在美国一方支付。这使我们能在一个小时内结算，而不是一到两天。我们甚至不向企业 CFO 介绍 “稳定币”；我们推销 “Jeeves Instant Pay”。他们关心的是信任、可靠性和成本，因为我们建立了自己同时处理法币和稳定币的账本，所以可以向他们提供一个更优秀、更快速、更具成本效益的产品，而不需要他们理解底层的区块链技术。

**Q: You’ve managed to scale significantly while actually reducing your headcount. How has AI enabled this operational leverage?**
> **Q: 你们成功地大规模扩张，同时实际减少了员工人数。人工智能如何实现这种运营杠杆？**

A: AI has been the primary unlock for our efficiency. For example, our underwriting team is just four people, yet they are managing two to three billion dollars in total payment volume. Two and a half years ago, that would have required 15 people. We use AI for document ingestion, KYB (Know Your Business) processes, and fraud detection. We even built our own OCR machine because we discovered that in Brazil, commas and dots in financial documents are often used interchangeably, which can lead to massive errors if a standard model is used. By training our own models on these specific regional nuances and automating tasks like GL coding—where an agent now labels transactions with 99% accuracy—we have grown our revenue 10x while our total headcount dropped from 200 to 140.
> A: 人工智能是我们效率的主要解锁因素。例如，我们的承保团队仅有四人，却管理着 20 至 30 亿美元的总支付量。两个半年前，这需要 15 个人。我们使用人工智能进行文档摄取、KYB（了解您的企业）流程和欺诈检测。我们甚至构建了自己的 OCR 机器，因为我们发现，在巴西，财务文件中的逗号和点常常交替使用，如果使用标准模型，可以导致大量错误。通过在这些特定区域的细微差别上训练我们的模型，并自动化任务如 GL 编码——现代理员以 99% 的准确率标记交易——我们使收入增长了 10 倍，而总员工人数从 200 人减少到 140 人。

**Q: You took a contrarian approach by targeting mid-market and enterprise companies early on rather than starting with smaller businesses. Why was that the right move?**
> **Q: 你们早期采取了对抗性的策略，瞄准中型市场和企业公司，而不是从小型企业开始。为什么这是正确的举动？**

A: We realized in 2023 that we weren't generating enough revenue from smaller businesses to justify the support costs, and we were essentially trying to do everything for everyone. We made the hard decision to focus exclusively on mid-market and enterprise companies. These clients allow us to cross-sell multiple products—like payments, treasury, and expense management—which significantly increases retention and gross profit. We now have strict onboarding requirements; if a company is below a certain revenue size, we simply don't onboard them. This focus forced us to build a much more robust, enterprise-grade product suite, and I don't think we would be where we are today if we hadn't made that difficult pivot.
> A: 我们在 2023 年意识到，从小型企业获得的收入不足以证明支持成本的合理性，我们本质上是在为每个人做所有事情。我们做出了困难的决定，专注于中型市场和企业公司。这些客户让我们能够交叉销售多种产品——如支付、财务和费用管理——这显著提高了客户留存率和毛利。我们现在有严格的入职要求；如果一家公司低于某个收入规模，我们根本不接受他们。这种专注促使我们构建了更强大、更符合企业级标准的产品套件，如果没有做出这个困难的转变，我认为我们今天不会达到现在的成就。
