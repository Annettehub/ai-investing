# Q & A

**Q: Can you explain the "gigawatts to cash" economic model and how you manage the current scarcity of compute?**
> **Q: 你能解释一下 “千瓦特到现金” 的经济模型以及你如何管理当前的计算资源稀缺吗？**

A: We are currently facing a vertical wall of demand where there simply aren't enough tokens available to satisfy the market. The "gigawatts to cash" model reflects the reality that compute is a scarce, high-value resource. We have to invest ahead of demand, which is why we have been aggressive in securing power, land, and chips. While compute is expensive, we see a massive deflationary curve on the cost of intelligence. For example, the deprecation cost from model 5 to 5.4 was roughly 97%. By leaning into this cost profile and building for the future, we ensure that as we scale, the per-token cost drops, making our services more efficient and valuable to customers.
> A: 我们目前正面临一堵需求的垂直墙，市场上根本没有足够的代币来满足需求。“千瓦特到现金” 模型反映了计算资源稀缺且价值高的现实。我们必须提前投资以应对需求，这也是我们积极确保电力、土地和芯片的原因。尽管计算资源昂贵，但我们看到智能的成本呈现出大幅的通货紧缩曲线。例如，从模型 5 到 5.4 的折旧成本大约为 97%。通过利用这一成本特征并为未来做准备，我们确保随着规模的扩大，单个代币的成本降低，使我们的服务对客户而言更高效、更有价值。

---

**Q: There is a perception that AI companies need to IPO early to maintain an advantage. How do you view the race to IPO?**
> **Q: 大家普遍认为 AI 公司需要尽早上市以保持竞争优势。你如何看待这场 IPO 竞赛？**

A: An IPO is a milestone, not a destination. I tell our team constantly not to run the company as if the IPO is the end goal. We raised $122 billion in March specifically to give ourselves maximum flexibility and optionality. My job as a CFO is to create that optionality for the company and this era of AI. The market is a weighing machine, not a popularity machine; no one remembers who went first in historical rivalries like Google versus Yahoo or Lyft versus Uber. Ultimately, we are focused on building a large, sustainable, and durable company, and fundraising is just a key component of that process.
> A: IPO 是一个里程碑，而不是最终目标。我不断告诉我们的团队，不要将公司经营视为 IPO 是终极目标。我们在三月份筹集了 1220 亿美元，明确是为了给自己最大的灵活性和选择权。作为 CFO，我的工作就是为公司和这个 AI 时代创造这种选择权。市场是一个称重机，而不是一个受欢迎程度的机器；没有人会记得历史上谁在谷歌与雅虎或 Lyft 与 Uber 的竞争中首个上市。最终，我们专注于建立一个大型、可持续和持久的公司，融资只是这一过程的一个关键组成部分。

---

**Q: Was there a mistake in not focusing enough on the enterprise side of the business over the last year?**
> **Q: 在过去一年中，不足够关注企业业务是否是一个错误？**

A: No, that is a false binary. We are not just a consumer company or an enterprise company; we are both. Our revenue is currently quite balanced, sitting at about 50-50. We are incredibly focused on the enterprise, working with major entities like Thermo Fisher and various banks to integrate AI into their workflows. However, we cannot leave the consumer behind because our mission is AGI for the benefit of all humanity, not just those who can pay enterprise premiums. We offer free tiers because we want people to experience intelligence; our data shows that as users move from free to paid tiers, their engagement increases significantly, similar to how the utility of a mobile phone evolved over time.
> A: 不是，这是一种错误的二元对立。我们不仅是一家消费者公司，也是一家企业公司；我们两者兼具。我们目前的收入相对均衡，约为 50-50。我们对企业非常关注，与 Thermo Fisher 和各大银行等主要机构合作，将 AI 集成到他们的工作流程中。然而，我们不能将消费者抛在后面，因为我们的使命是为全人类带来 AGI，而不仅仅是为那些能够支付企业溢价的用户提供。我们提供免费层级，因为我们希望人们体验智能；我们的数据显示，用户从免费层级转向付费层级时，他们的参与度显著提高，这类似于移动电话的实用性随着时间的推移而演变。

**Q: How do you approach the "supply chain of trust" when deploying massive data centers in local communities?**
> **Q: 在地方社区部署大型数据中心时，你如何看待 “信任供应链”？**

A: Trust is a critical part of our supply chain. You cannot dictate to a community what they need from the top down. When we build a data center, such as our project in Saline, Michigan, we engage directly with the local community to ensure we are providing value. We commit to not raising local electricity rates, we create 2,500 union jobs, we pay significant taxes, and we invest millions in local education and training. It is about ensuring that we don't leave communities behind as we build out this infrastructure.
> A: 信任是我们供应链的关键部分。你不能从上到下地告诉一个社区他们需要什么。当我们建立数据中心，例如我们在密歇根州萨林的项目时，我们直接与当地社区接洽，以确保提供价值。我们承诺不提高当地的电价，创造 2500 个工会职位，支付大量税款，并投资数百万用于当地教育和培训。确保在我们建设这个基础设施时不让社区落后是至关重要的。

---

**Q: How do you think about capital allocation and your portfolio approach to ensure you are capturing the biggest returns?**
> **Q: 你如何看待资本分配和投资组合策略，以确保你获取最大的回报？**

A: We approach our strategy like a Rubik's Cube, which allows for maximum optionality. We have moved from relying on a single cloud service provider and a single chip manufacturer to a multi-dimensional strategy. We now work with every major cloud provider—Microsoft, Oracle, CoreWeave, GCP, and AWS—which allows us to shift CapEx into OpEx. On the hardware side, we are multi-chip, prioritizing NVIDIA while also integrating AMD, Cerebras, and our own custom silicon with Broadcom. This diversification ensures that we are never locked into a single point of failure and can always remain on the frontier of performance.
> A: 我们将我们的战略视为一个魔方，最大限度地提供选择权。我们已经从依赖单一的云服务提供商和单一的芯片制造商转变为多维战略。我们现在与每个主要的云服务提供商——微软、甲骨文、CoreWeave、GCP 和 AWS 合作，这使我们能够将资本支出转入运营支出。在硬件方面，我们采用多芯片策略，优先考虑 NVIDIA，同时整合 AMD、Cerebras 以及我们自己的 Broadcom 定制硅。这种多样化确保我们不会被锁定在单一点故障中，并且永远能够保持在性能的最前沿。

---

**Q: What is the vision for the next generation of AI consumer devices, and how will they change the way we interact with technology?**
> **Q: 下一代 AI 消费设备的愿景是什么，它将如何改变我们与科技的互动方式？**

A: We are moving toward a consumer substrate that brings humanity to devices. Current technology is often mechanistic, but great design makes the interface fade away. We are focusing on multimodality—moving away from the "thumb-based" interaction we were taught by the previous generation of mobile technology. We want users to talk to their tools naturally. I have seen our upcoming consumer project, and it feels very natural and, most importantly, lovable. It is about creating an intimate, seamless experience that doesn't feel like you are just staring at a screen.
> A: 我们正朝着一种能够为设备带来人性的消费基础设施发展。当前的技术往往是机械的，但伟大的设计让界面淡去。我们专注于多模态——走 away from the “thumb-based” interaction, 也就是之前一代移动技术所教的交互方式。我们希望用户能够自然地与他们的工具对话。我看过我们即将推出的消费项目，它感觉非常自然，最重要的是让人喜爱。我们旨在创造一种亲密、无缝的体验，不让人感觉自己只是在盯着屏幕。

**Q: Is an advertising model the solution to making your services free for the world?**
> **Q: 广告模型是否是让你的服务免费向全世界提供的解决方案？**

A: We want to stick to our principles, which means ensuring that users always get the best result based on the model, not based on something that was sponsored. We will always maintain an ad-free tier for those who prefer it. However, if Google and Meta had a baby, it would be ChatGPT. We have high-intent search traffic, and because we have a memory of who the user is and what they care about, we can combine search intent with deep context. This makes for a very potent advertising platform that could provide the revenue necessary to offer broad access to AI, but we are currently prioritizing the API and enterprise utility because that is where we see the most immediate value creation.
> A: 我们希望坚持我们的原则，这意味着确保用户总是根据模型获得最佳结果，而不是基于某种赞助的东西。我们会始终为那些更喜欢的用户保留无广告层级。但是，如果谷歌和 Meta 有一个孩子，那就是 ChatGPT。我们有高意图的搜索流量，因而我们记得用户是谁以及他们关心的事情，我们可以将搜索意图与深度背景结合。这使得我们打造了一个非常强大的广告平台，能够提供必要的收入，以便为 AI 提供广泛的访问，但我们目前优先考虑 API 和企业实用性，因为我们认为那是创造最直接价值的地方。
