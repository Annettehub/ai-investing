# Summary

Prediction markets serve as powerful mechanisms for aggregating information and guiding high-stakes decision-making, moving beyond mere public discourse or sports betting. Robin Hanson argues that these markets, when applied to organizational and personal choices—such as evaluating CEO performance or life outcomes—provide objective, hard-to-manipulate data that mitigates agency problems. While current platforms like Polymarket and Kalshi face regulatory backlash and accusations of gambling, their growth lowers infrastructure costs and establishes legal precedents necessary for broader adoption. Historically, financial markets faced similar skepticism before gaining legitimacy through demonstrated value. Beyond information aggregation, these markets offer a structured space for competitive expression, mirroring the role of games in human social coordination. Future applications could revolutionize how companies manage mergers, restructuring, and capital allocation, ultimately transforming prediction markets into essential tools for rational, data-driven decision-making across various sectors.
> 预测市场作为一种强大的机制，在整合信息和指导高风险决策方面发挥着作用，其意义已超越了单纯的公共话语或体育博彩。罗宾·汉森认为，当这些市场应用于组织和个人选择——例如评估首席执行官的绩效或人生结果——时，它们能提供客观、难以操纵的数据，从而缓解代理问题。虽然目前像 Polymarket 和 Kalshi 这样的平台面临监管阻力和赌博指控，但其增长降低了基础设施成本，并为更广泛的采用建立了必要的法律先例。历史上，金融市场在通过展现价值获得合法性之前，也曾面临类似的质疑。除了信息整合，这些市场还为竞争性表达提供了一个结构化空间，反映了游戏在人类社会协作中的作用。未来的应用可能会彻底改变公司管理合并、重组和资本配置的方式，最终将预测市场转变为各行业中理性、数据驱动决策的重要工具。

# Takeaways

* Prediction markets offer a superior mechanism for organizational decision-making by providing objective, hard-to-manipulate data that bypasses the biases inherent in traditional reporting and lobbying.
  > 预测市场通过提供客观且难以操纵的数据，绕过了传统报告和游说机制中固有的偏见，从而为组织决策提供了一种更优的机制。
* Conditional stock markets—where share prices fluctuate based on specific outcomes like a CEO’s tenure—can effectively resolve the manager-agent problem by offering clear, market-driven advice on high-stakes corporate actions.
  > 条件股票市场——即股价根据特定结果（如首席执行官的任期）波动——可以通过提供明确且由市场驱动的建议，有效解决高风险企业行为中的委托代理问题，。
* Financial and betting markets function as "sub-worlds" that allow individuals to safely express competitive and aggressive instincts that are typically suppressed in standard social environments.
  > 金融和博彩市场作为 “子世界” 运作，使个人能够安全地表达在标准社交环境中通常受到抑制的竞争和进取本能。
* The legitimacy of financial instruments like stocks and insurance is a result of long-term social acceptance rather than inherent moral superiority, as these markets were historically viewed as illegal gambling.
  > 股票和保险等金融工具的合法性是长期社会接受的结果，而非其本身具有道德优越性，因为这些市场在历史上曾被视为非法赌博。
* Human social coordination relies on the creation of distinct "sub-worlds" with specific rules, and prediction markets represent a new, specialized sub-world for aggregating information.
  > 人类的社会协作依赖于创建具有特定规则的独特 “子世界”，而预测市场代表了一个用于聚合信息的新型专业子世界。
* Detailed, technical forecasting of future scenarios is a viable intellectual exercise that can produce accurate insights, challenging the common assumption that the future is too complex to be modeled.
  > 对未来情景进行详尽、技术性的预测是一种可行的智力活动，能够产生准确的见解，从而挑战了关于未来过于复杂而无法建模的普遍假设。
* The current dominance of sports betting in prediction market volume risks triggering a "prudish" regulatory backlash that could stifle the development of more valuable, serious decision-making applications.
  > 目前体育博彩在预测市场交易量中的主导地位，可能会引发 “道学” 式的监管反弹，从而抑制更有价值的严肃决策应用的发展。

# Q & A

**Q: How do prediction markets move beyond public news to provide real-world value?**
> **Q: 预测市场如何超越公共新闻，提供现实世界的价值？**

A: The basic vision is that speculative markets are unmatched mechanisms for aggregating information. While most people initially think of markets for public conversation topics like news or politics, the real value lies in advising decisions for individuals and organizations. For example, we can use conditional stock markets to advise on major business ventures—such as whether to keep a CEO—by comparing the company's stock price under different leadership outcomes. This provides objective, market-driven advice on high-stakes decisions.
> A: 其基本愿景是，投机市场是聚合信息的无与伦比的机制。虽然大多数人最初想到的是新闻或政治等公共话题的市场，但真正的价值在于为个人和组织的决策提供建议。例如，我们可以利用条件性股票市场来为重大商业决策提供建议——比如是否保留首席执行官——通过比较公司在不同领导结果下的股价。这为高风险决策提供了客观的、市场驱动的建议。

---

**Q: Why is there a push to criminalize prediction markets, such as the new law in Minnesota?**
> **Q: 为什么要推动将预测市场定为刑事犯罪，例如明尼苏达州的新法律？**

A: This is partly a reaction to the shift from state-regulated sports betting to national-level competition, which threatens certain economic interests. More broadly, there is a persistent, "prudish" cultural perception that views these markets as either wasteful gambling or an immoral way to profit from tragedy. This backlash risks shutting down the infrastructure and legal precedents needed for the more promising, high-value applications of this technology.
> A: 这在一定程度上是对从州监管的体育博彩向国家级竞争转变的反应，这种转变威胁到了某些经济利益。更广泛地说，存在一种持续的、甚至 “拘谨” 的文化观念，将这些市场视为浪费性的赌博，或是一种通过悲剧获利的不道德方式。这种抵制可能会导致我们关闭该技术所需的基础设施和法律先例，而这些对于更有前景、高价值的应用至关重要。

---

**Q: How can prediction markets solve the manager-agent problem within companies?**
> **Q: 预测市场如何解决公司内部的管理者与代理人问题？**

A: Corporate decisions are often heavily influenced by lobbying and biased internal reports. Markets are remarkably neutral and hard to manipulate. By creating a market that reflects the company's value conditional on a specific decision—like firing a CEO—the board receives objective, market-aggregated information that is much harder to bias than a report written by an interested party.
> A: 企业决策往往深受游说和带有偏见的内部报告影响。而市场是非常中立且难以操纵的。通过创建一个反映公司在特定决策（如解雇首席执行官）条件下价值的市场，董事会可以获得客观的、由市场聚合的信息，这比由利益相关方撰写的报告要难操纵得多。

---

**Q: Why does sports betting dwarf other prediction markets in terms of trading volume?**
> **Q: 为什么体育博彩在交易量方面远远超过其他预测市场？**

A: Sports have long been one of the few socially accepted outlets for human aggression and competitiveness. People want to prove themselves, and betting allows them to take an aggressive stance on a team or event they are already invested in. This combination of fandom, the desire for action, and the opportunity to test one's knowledge makes sports betting a natural, high-volume application of speculative markets.
> A: 长期以来，体育一直是人类发泄攻击性和竞争意识的少数社会认可渠道之一。人们想要证明自己，而下注让他们能够对自己已经投入感情的球队或赛事采取激进的立场。这种粉丝文化、对行动的渴望以及测试自身知识的机会的结合，使得体育博彩成为投机市场中一种自然且高交易量的应用。

---

**Q: How do games and betting markets fit into your theory of human social behavior?**
> **Q: 游戏和博彩市场如何契合你的人类社会行为理论？**

A: Humans constantly create "sub-worlds" with their own sets of rules to manage social life—like the specific etiquette of a dinner party. Games and betting markets are essentially bounded sub-worlds that allow us to express aggression and competitiveness in a way that is restricted and safe. We have a deep, innate need to be aggressive and prove ourselves, and these markets provide a structured environment to do that without violating broader social norms.
> A: 人类不断创造出带有自己规则的 “子世界” 来管理社会生活——比如晚宴的特定礼仪。游戏和博彩市场本质上是有边界的子世界，让我们能够以一种受限且安全的方式表达攻击性和竞争性。我们有着深刻而天生的、需要表现攻击性并证明自己的需求，而这些市场提供了一个结构化的环境，让我们在不违反更广泛社会规范的情况下实现这一点。
