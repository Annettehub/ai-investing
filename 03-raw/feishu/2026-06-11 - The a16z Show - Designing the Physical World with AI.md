# Summary

Physical world AI is bridging the gap between digital design and physical execution by treating hardware and infrastructure projects as code-based systems. By leveraging AI to automate complex design permutations and manufacturing processes, firms like Diode Computers and Unlimited Industries are drastically reducing timelines for circuit board production and large-scale infrastructure projects. This transition relies on shifting from manual, fragmented workflows to model-led architectures that optimize for total cost of ownership rather than just engineering costs. While data scarcity remains a significant hurdle, the integration of simulation tools and the codification of design primitives allow AI to develop the intuition necessary for autonomous production. Ultimately, these advancements aim to re-industrialize the United States by making the creation of complex physical systems as accessible and rapid as software development, effectively lowering the barrier to entry for ambitious hardware innovation.
> 物理世界的人工智能正在通过将硬件和基础设施项目视为基于代码的系统，弥合数字设计与物理执行之间的差距。通过利用人工智能来自动化复杂的设计变化和制造过程，像二极管计算机和无限工业这样的公司正在大幅缩短电路板生产和大规模基础设施项目的时间线。这一转变依赖于从手动、碎片化的工作流程转向以模型为主导的架构，这些架构优化整体拥有成本，而不仅仅是工程成本。虽然数据稀缺仍然是一个重大障碍，但模拟工具的集成和设计原语的编码使人工智能能够发展出自主生产所需的直觉。最终，这些进展旨在通过使复杂物理系统的创建与软件开发一样可访问和快速，从而重新实现美国的工业化，有效降低雄心勃勃的硬件创新的准入门槛。

# Takeaways

* Treating physical design as code enables AI to leverage existing software-based LLM capabilities to solve hardware and infrastructure problems, effectively bypassing the need for specialized, non-existent training data.
  > 将物理设计视为代码使得人工智能能够利用现有的软件基础大型语言模型的能力来解决硬件和基础设施问题，有效地绕过对特殊、非现存训练数据的需求。
* To disrupt traditional, slow-moving industries like construction and hardware manufacturing, startups must vertically integrate enough of the process to provide a clean, familiar interface to customers rather than forcing them to adopt entirely new software frameworks.
  > 为了打破建筑和硬件制造等传统、缓慢发展的行业，初创公司必须对足够的流程进行垂直整合，以便为客户提供一个干净、熟悉的接口，而不是迫使他们采用全新的软件框架。
* The primary barrier to innovation in large-scale infrastructure is not merely technical, but the risk-averse nature of project finance, which prioritizes stable returns over the adoption of new, unproven technologies.
  > 大型基础设施创新的主要障碍不仅仅是技术性问题，而是项目融资的规避风险特性，优先考虑稳定的回报而非采用新的、未经验证的技术。
* The lack of standardized, digitized design data for physical systems remains the critical bottleneck preventing AI from fully automating the end-to-end lifecycle of hardware and construction projects.
  > 缺乏针对物理系统的标准化、数字化设计数据仍然是阻碍人工智能完全实现硬件和建筑项目端到端生命周期自动化的关键瓶颈。
* Shifting simulation from an inference-time verification step to a training-time tool allows models to develop the "intuition" necessary to handle complex physical constraints autonomously, rather than relying on human-led verification.
  > 将仿真从推理时的验证步骤转变为训练时的工具，使模型能够发展出独立处理复杂物理约束所需的 “直觉”，而不是依赖人-led 验证。
* While 80% of manufacturing and construction can be automated with existing robotics, the remaining 20% requires a fundamental shift in design philosophy to ensure the final output is inherently manufacturable without manual intervention.
  > 虽然现有的机器人可自动化 80% 的制造和建筑，但剩余的 20% 需要对设计理念进行根本性的转变，以确保最终输出本质上是可制造的，无需人工干预。
* Designing systems for full autonomy from the start, rather than relying on human-in-the-loop workflows, is essential for driving the technical architecture required to scale physical world AI.
  > 从一开始就为完全自动化设计系统，而非依赖人类参与的工作流程，是推动所需技术架构以扩大物理世界人工智能规模的关键。
* The ability to rapidly iterate on physical designs—treating them as updated variables rather than static blueprints—drastically improves the financial viability of large-scale projects by reducing the cost of mid-cycle changes.
  > 快速迭代物理设计的能力——将其视为更新变量而非静态蓝图——通过降低中期变化的成本，大幅提高了大规模项目的财务可行性。

> **Q: 你声称在 10 年内，所有建筑将实现全面自动化；这实际上是什么样子，需要什么来实现这一目标？**

A: A construction project, like a power plant or hospital, currently spends 12 to 18 months in the design phase, involving hundreds of engineers and fragmented workflows. We are automating the end-to-end creation of the "Issued for Construction" (IFC) package. By feeding site requirements into an AI, we can explore tens of thousands of design permutations at the click of a button to produce a globally optimized IFC package. The second half of the equation involves robotics—ranging from autonomous earthmovers to humanoid systems on-site. The key is aligning incentives; currently, the industry is driven by project finance, which prioritizes stable, risk-averse returns, often stifling innovation. To reach full automation, we must design systems to be inherently autonomous rather than just "human-in-the-loop."
> A: 一个建筑项目，比如发电厂或医院，目前在设计阶段花费 12 到 18 个月，涉及数百名工程师和零散的工作流程。我们正在自动化 “施工许可”（IFC）包的端到端创建。通过将现场要求输入到人工智能中，我们可以在点击按钮的瞬间探索数万个设计变体，生成全球优化的 IFC 包。方程的后半部分涉及机器人——从自主的土方机械到现场的人形系统。关键是对齐激励机制；目前，行业受项目融资驱动，优先考虑稳定、保守的回报，往往抑制创新。要实现全面自动化，我们必须设计出固有自主的系统，而不仅仅是 “人以环路”。

**Q: How are you using AI to transform the design and manufacturing of custom circuit boards?**
> **Q: 你们如何利用人工智能来改变定制电路板的设计和制造？**

A: We treat hardware design as a software problem. Traditional electronics design is not code-based, but we built a compiler that gives the AI enough hints to feel like it is writing a Python program rather than designing a circuit board. This allows us to bridge the gap between design and manufacturing. While the surface-mount technology (SMT) process is already highly automated, about 20% of assembly—like large transformers or non-standard components—remains manual. Our goal is to constrain the design so that it is fully manufacturable via 100% automation. We aren't just selling software; we are delivering a physical product faster and cheaper by owning the infrastructure, which allows us to fit into the existing mold of how enterprise customers already buy hardware.
> A: 我们将硬件设计视为一个软件问题。传统的电子设计不是基于代码的，但我们建立了一个编译器，使人工智能能感受到它正在编写 Python 程序，而不是设计电路板。这使我们能够弥合设计与制造之间的差距。虽然表面贴装技术（SMT）过程已经高度自动化，但大约 20% 的组装——例如大型变压器或非标准组件——仍然是手动的。我们的目标是限制设计，使其能够通过 100% 自动化完全制造。我们不仅在销售软件；通过拥有基础设施，我们提供更快、更便宜的实体产品，这使我们能够融入企业客户购买硬件的现有模式。

**Q: Why is vertical integration necessary when trying to modernize an industry as traditional as construction?**

A: You have to earn the right to innovate. The construction industry is notoriously resistant to change, and the incentive structures are tied to project finance, which doesn't reward taking risks on new technology. If you try to pick off one small part of the workflow, you force people to change their habits, which they are often unwilling to do. By vertically integrating—owning a large enough portion of the design, engineering, procurement, and construction process—we create a clean interface for the industry. We provide a finished product that the customer already knows how to buy, while we handle the implementation details of the AI-driven automation behind the scenes.
> A: 你必须赢得创新的权利。建筑行业 notoriously 抵制变化，激励机制与项目融资挂钩，这并不奖励对新技术的风险投资。如果你试图剥离工作流程中的一小部分，你迫使人们改变习惯，而他们通常不愿意这样做。通过垂直整合——拥有设计、工程、采购和施工过程中的一个足够大部分——我们为行业创建了一个清晰的接口。我们提供一个客户已经知道如何购买的成品，同时我们处理 AI 驱动的自动化背后的实施细节。

## Vertical Integration Strategies to Overcome Entrenched Industry Resistance
> 克服根深蒂固的行业抵抗的垂直整合策略

Traditional industries like construction and hardware manufacturing are often resistant to new software tools due to high switching costs and misaligned incentives. Rather than selling software, companies are finding success by delivering the end product itself, fitting into existing procurement molds while implementing AI-driven internal processes. This vertical integration allows for a clean interface with the industry, bypassing the need to convince traditional firms to adopt new, unproven software frameworks. By owning the infrastructure and providing transparency into the design process, these firms can demonstrate superior speed and cost-efficiency, effectively earning the right to expand their scope and influence within the sector.
> 建筑和硬件制造等传统行业常常因为高昂的转换成本和不匹配的激励而抵制新的软件工具。与其出售软件，公司成功地通过交付最终产品本身融入现有采购模式，同时实施 AI 驱动的内部流程。这种垂直整合允许与行业建立清晰的界面，绕过说服传统公司采用新的、未经验证的软件框架的必要性。通过拥有基础设施并提供设计过程的透明度，这些公司可以展示更高的速度和成本效率，从而有效地赢得扩大其在行业内范围和影响力的权利。
