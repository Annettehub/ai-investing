# Summary

本期播客探讨了在 AI 时代，科技市场底层逻辑发生的深刻变化，以及对未来科技发展趋势的判断。播客认为，当前并非处于互联网泡沫时期，理由是 AI 基础设施建设主要由现金流充沛的科技巨头主导，而非依赖债务投机的公司。AI 的市场潜力巨大，不仅限于替代现有软件，更能渗透并替代部分白领工作，市场天花板是白领薪资市场。随着模型成本的下降，AI 应用将迎来繁荣，不应过分关注短期毛利率，而应关注其解决实际问题的能力。此外，能源将成为算力的关键，掌握能源者将掌握算力的命门。
> This episode of the podcast explores the profound changes in the underlying logic of the technology market in the AI era, as well as judgments about future technology development trends. The podcast argues that we are currently not in an internet bubble, as the construction of AI infrastructure is primarily led by cash-rich technology giants rather than companies that rely on debt speculation. The market potential of AI is enormous, not only limited to replacing existing software but also capable of permeating and replacing certain white-collar jobs, with the market ceiling being the white-collar salary market. As the cost of models decreases, AI applications will enter a period of prosperity, and we should not overly focus on short-term gross margins but rather on their ability to solve real problems. Furthermore, energy will become key to computing power, with those who control energy holding the critical link to computing power.

# Takeaways

* 科技公司已占据标普 500 指数前列，表明科技不再是独立行业，而是已吞噬整个市场。
  > The tech companies have occupied the forefront of the S&P 500 index, indicating that technology is no longer an independent industry but has consumed the entire market.
* AI 基础设施建设规模超越历史上的任何工业建设，但其主要买单者是资产负债表健康的科技巨头，降低了系统性金融风险。
  > The scale of AI infrastructure construction has surpassed any industrial development in history, with the main payers being financially healthy tech giants, reducing systemic financial risk.
* AI 模型的成本在过去两年里下降超过 99%，而模型能力却在快速进化，为应用层的爆发积蓄了势能。
  > The cost of AI models has dropped by more than 99% in the past two years, while model capabilities are rapidly evolving, accumulating potential for an explosion at the application layer.
* AI 的市场天花板远超现有软件，它将渗透、辅助甚至替代白领工作，市场规模可达美国 GDP 的 20%。
  > The market ceiling for AI far exceeds existing software; it will penetrate, assist, and even replace white-collar work, with a market size potentially reaching 20% of the U.S. GDP.
* ChatGPT 的普及速度是谷歌的 5.5 倍，因为 AI 站在了互联网和云计算的肩膀上，分发速度前所未有。
  > The adoption speed of ChatGPT is 5.5 times that of Google because AI stands on the shoulders of the internet and cloud computing, distributing at an unprecedented speed.
* AI 公司以低廉价格释放巨大价值，未来商业模式将从人头费转向更复杂的定价策略和精准推荐。
  > AI companies release immense value at low prices, and the future business model will shift from per capita fees to more complex pricing strategies and precise recommendations.
* 随着模型供应商之间竞争加剧和硬件性能提升，AI 应用的毛利率将会自然修复。
  > As competition intensifies between model suppliers and hardware performance improves, the gross margins for AI applications will naturally recover.
* AI 应用深度嵌入工作流，解决具体痛点，用户粘性高，例如 AI 自动生成医疗问诊记录。
  > AI applications will be deeply embedded in workflows to address specific pain points, resulting in high user stickiness, such as AI automatically generating medical consultation records.
* 未来 AI 软件将是主动和预测性的，通过观察工作流和接入非结构化数据，自动帮助用户完成任务。
  > Future AI software will be proactive and predictive, automatically assisting users in task completion by observing workflows and accessing unstructured data.
* 能源将是下一个十年的硬通货，谁掌握了能源，谁就掌握了算力的命门。
  > Energy will be the hard currency of the next decade; whoever masters energy will have control over the power of computing.

# Q & A

**Q: a16z 认为当前 AI 领域是否处于泡沫之中？他们的理由是什么？**
> **Q: Does a16z believe the current AI field is in a bubble? What are their reasons?**

A: a16z 认为当前 AI 领域并没有处于泡沫之中。他们的主要理由是，当前 AI 基础设施的建设是由现金流充沛的科技巨头主导的，例如谷歌、微软和 Meta。这些公司有能力承担大规模的投入，甚至可以承受一定程度的产能过剩。这与 2000 年互联网泡沫时期由负债累累的电信公司主导的基础设施建设不同，后者一旦需求达不到预期，就会面临破产风险，从而引发系统性风险。
> A: a16z believes that the current AI field is not in a bubble. Their main reason is that the current construction of AI infrastructure is dominated by cash-rich tech giants like Google, Microsoft, and Meta. These companies have the capability to make large-scale investments and can even withstand a certain degree of overcapacity. This is different from the infrastructure construction during the 2000 internet bubble which was led by indebted telecom companies that faced bankruptcy risks once demand did not meet expectations, leading to systemic risks.

---

**Q: a16z 如何看待 AI 的市场潜力？**
> **Q: How does a16z view the market potential of AI?**

A: a16z 认为 AI 的市场潜力远大于传统软件市场。他们指出，美国企业软件市场的总支出大约占美国 GDP 的 1%，而 AI 所能影响的领域，即美国白领的薪资总额，占到了 GDP 的 20%。这意味着 AI 的市场天花板不仅仅是替代现有的软件，而是去渗透、辅助，甚至在某些领域替代那 20% 的白领工作产出。这是一场从工具到劳动力的跨越。
> A: a16z believes that the market potential of AI is far greater than that of the traditional software market. They point out that the total spending of the U.S. enterprise software market accounts for about 1% of the U.S. GDP, while the areas AI can influence, specifically the total salary of U.S. white-collar workers, accounts for 20% of GDP. This means that the market ceiling for AI is not just about replacing existing software but about penetrating, assisting, and even replacing that 20% of white-collar job output in certain areas. This represents a leap from tools to labor.

**Q: 为什么现在 AI 技术的普及速度如此之快？**
> **Q: Why is the speed of AI technology adoption so fast now?**

A: AI 技术的普及速度之所以如此之快，是因为它站在了巨人的肩膀上。互联网和云计算已经完成了全球范围内的连接和算力铺设，地球上超过一半的人口已经拥有了智能手机。AI 不需要像当年的互联网那样先去铺设网线，再去教用户怎么用电脑，它只需要通过云端瞬间就可以分发到全球数十亿用户的指尖。这种分发速度是人类商业史上前所未有的。
> A: The reason the adoption speed of AI technology is so fast is that it stands on the shoulders of giants. The internet and cloud computing have completed global connectivity and computational power infrastructure, with more than half of the world's population now owning smartphones. AI does not need to lay down wires as the internet did back in the day and then teach users how to use computers. Instead, it can be instantly distributed to billions of users worldwide through the cloud. This distribution speed is unprecedented in human commercial history.

**Q: a16z 如何看待当前 AI 产品的定价策略和消费者剩余问题？**
> **Q: How does a16z view the pricing strategies of current AI products and the issue of consumer surplus?**

A: a16z 认为，目前的 AI 公司其实是在以非常低廉的价格向市场释放着巨大的价值。例如，ChatGPT Plus 或 Cloud Pro 这样的订阅服务，一个月大约是 20 美元，但它们提供的价值对于重度用户来说远远超过 20 美元。甚至有用户表示，哪怕涨到 200 美元一个月，只要能够大幅提升工作效率，他们也会毫不犹豫地买单。这意味着 AI 公司尚未完全榨取消费者剩余，未来的商业模式可能会从简单的人头费模式转向更复杂的定价策略，甚至是广告模式的变种。
> A: a16z believes that current AI companies are actually releasing significant value to the market at very low prices. For example, subscription services like ChatGPT Plus or Cloud Pro cost about $20 per month, but the value they provide far exceeds $20 for heavy users. Some users even claim that they would not hesitate to pay $200 per month if it could significantly enhance their work efficiency. This means that AI companies have not yet fully extracted consumer surplus, and future business models may shift from simple headcount fee models to more complex pricing strategies, or even variations of advertising models.

**Q: a16z 如何看待 AI 应用公司的毛利率问题？**
> **Q: How does a16z view the gross margin issues of AI application companies?**

A: a16z 认为，不能用看待传统软件的眼光来看待 AI 应用。他们预测，随着模型供应商之间竞争加剧以及硬件性能的提升，推理成本必然会大幅度下降，AI 应用的毛利率将会自然修复。更重要的是，对于一个能够解决复杂问题，甚至替代部分人类工作的 AI agent 来说，如果他能够为你节省一个年薪 10 万美元的工程师，那么即使他的软件毛利率稍微低一点，对于客户来说也是非常划算的。客户看重的是结果的交付，而不是单纯的软件租赁。
> A: a16z believes that AI applications should not be viewed with the same lens as traditional software. They predict that as competition among model providers intensifies and hardware performance improves, inference costs will inevitably drop significantly, leading to a natural recovery in the gross margins of AI applications. More importantly, for an AI agent that can solve complex problems or even replace parts of human labor, if it can save you the salary of a $100,000 engineer, then even if its software gross margin is slightly lower, it is still very cost-effective for customers. Customers care about the delivery of results, not just leasing software.

**Q: a16z 认为 AI 公司构建护城河的关键是什么？**
> **Q: What does a16z believe is the key for AI companies to build a moat?**

A: a16z 认为，AI 公司构建护城河的关键在于用户体验的彻底重构。未来的 AI 软件应该是主动的、预测性的，它不应该等着你去输入数据，而应该通过观察你的工作流，接入你的非结构化数据，自动帮助你完成任务。这种从记录系统到行动系统的转变，将是初创公司挑战传统巨头的最大的机会。
> A: a16z believes that the key for AI companies to build a moat is the complete reconstruction of user experience. Future AI software should be proactive and predictive; it should not wait for you to input data but should automatically help you complete tasks by observing your workflow and accessing your unstructured data. This transformation from record systems to action systems will be the biggest opportunity for startups to challenge traditional giants.

**Q: a16z 认为未来 AI 发展面临的最大瓶颈是什么？**
> **Q: What does a16z believe is the biggest bottleneck facing the future development of AI?**

A: a16z 认为未来 AI 发展面临的最大瓶颈可能不是算力，也不是算法，而是能源。数据中心是吃电的巨兽，目前的电网承载能力可能跟不上 AI 算力的指数级扩张。这也是为什么我们看到微软重启三里岛的核电站，看到各大科技巨头都在疯狂的投资清洁能源。谁掌握了能源，谁就掌握了算力的命门。
> A: a16z believes that the biggest bottleneck facing the future development of AI may not be computing power or algorithms, but energy. Data centers are significant consumers of electricity, and the current grid capacity may not keep up with the exponential expansion of AI computing power. This is why we see Microsoft restarting the Three Mile Island nuclear power plant and why major tech giants are heavily investing in clean energy. Whoever masters energy will control the key to computing power.

**Q: a16z 观察到当前 AI 市场的结构是怎样的？**
> **Q: What has a16z observed about the current structure of the AI market?**

A: a16z 观察到，目前的市场呈现出一种极其明显的双峰结构。一边是那些非常稀缺拥有世界级团队的超级公司，比如 OpenAI、XAI、Databricks 等等，这些公司的创始人往往具备一种特殊的特质，被称为是硬核资本家的科学家，他们既懂得最前沿的技术，又拥有极强的商业野心和执行力。另一边则是大量普通的 SaaS 公司，他们正在面临着增长乏力的困境。
> A: a16z has observed that the current market exhibits an extremely obvious bimodal structure. On one end are super companies with world-class teams, such as OpenAI, XAI, and Databricks, whose founders often possess a unique trait described as scientists who are hardcore capitalists. They understand cutting-edge technology and possess strong business ambition and execution. On the other end lies a large number of ordinary SaaS companies, which are facing growth challenges.

**Q: a16z 的投资策略是什么？**
> **Q: What is a16z’s investment strategy?**

A: a16z 的策略非常明确，那就是非对称下注。他们宁愿在看起来很贵的阶段去投资那些最顶级的团队，因为这些公司的天花板非常高，成功的收益可以覆盖掉所有的风险。同时，他们也非常看好美国活力这个赛道，这是一个涵盖了国防、航空航天、先进制造等实体产业的概念。
> A: a16z's strategy is very clear: asymmetrical betting. They prefer to invest in the highest-level teams even when things seem expensive because these companies have very high ceilings, and the returns from successful outcomes can cover all risks. At the same time, they are also very optimistic about the U.S. vitality sector, which encompasses concepts from defense, aerospace, advanced manufacturing, and other physical industries.

**Q: 为什么在公开市场上感觉赚钱越来越难了？**
> **Q: Why does it feel like making money in the public market is getting harder?**

A: 高增长的公司正在以前所未有的速度在私有市场里完成他们的价值增值。以前，像亚马逊这样的公司上市时，市值可能只有几亿美元，普通股民可以陪它一起成长到万亿市值。但是现在，像 OpenAI 或者 Databricks，它们在上市前可能就已经是一千亿甚至几千亿美元的规模了。这意味着增长最快、最肥美的那一段余生被私有市场的投资者吃掉了，留给公开市场的往往是增长曲线已经开始平缓的阶段。
> A: High-growth companies are adding value in the private market at an unprecedented speed. In the past, companies like Amazon might have had a market value of only a few hundred million dollars at IPO, allowing ordinary shareholders to grow with it to a trillion-dollar valuation. However, now companies like OpenAI or Databricks may already be worth hundreds of billions even before going public. This means that the fastest-growing and most lucrative segments have been consumed by private market investors, leaving the public market with stages where growth curves have started to flatten.
