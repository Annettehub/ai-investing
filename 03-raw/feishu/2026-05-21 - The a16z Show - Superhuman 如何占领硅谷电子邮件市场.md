# Takeaways

* The "Superhuman question"—asking users how they would feel if the product disappeared—provides a quantifiable metric for product-market fit, where a 40% "very disappointed" threshold serves as a reliable indicator of growth potential.
  > “超人问题”——询问用户如果产品消失会有何感受——提供了一个可量化的产品市场契合度指标，其中 40% 的 “非常失望” 阈值作为增长潜力的可靠指标。
* Effective product design incorporates "toy-like" elements and "squishy affordances" that encourage playful exploration, which is fundamentally different from superficial gamification tactics like badges or leaderboards that can actually diminish intrinsic user motivation.
  > 有效的产品设计融入 “玩具般” 的元素和 “可挤压的可供性”，鼓励人们进行有趣的探索，这与像徽章或排行榜这样的表面化游戏化策略根本不同，后者实际上可能会降低用户的内在动机。

  > 高接触、手动入职作为一个战略工具，控制产品的 “爆炸半径”，使创始人能够直接观察用户工作流程，并在扩大到更广泛的受众之前消除 bug 疲劳。

* Exercising the "right to not serve" specific customer segments allows a company to maintain high product quality and focus, preventing negative feedback from users whose needs the product is not yet designed to meet.
  > 行使 “拒绝服务” 特定客户群体的权利，能够让公司保持高产品质量和专注，防止来自尚未设计满足需求的用户的负面反馈。

* Sustainable product growth relies on a balanced roadmap that allocates equal energy to enhancing core features that existing users love and systematically addressing the "boring" complaints that hinder wider adoption.
  > 可持续的产品增长依赖于一个平衡的路线图，平均分配精力来增强现有用户喜爱的核心功能，并系统地解决阻碍更广泛采用的 “无聊” 投诉。

  > “专业消费者” 市场细分——以拥有高经济能力但时间有限的强大用户为特征——代表了一个独特的机会，将消费级设计感应用于复杂的专业工作流程。

# Q & A

**Q: How can founders effectively apply game design principles to productivity software, and what is the key difference between game design and gamification?**
> **Q: 创始人如何有效地将游戏设计原则应用于生产力软件，以及游戏设计与游戏化之间的关键区别是什么？**

A: Gamification—using points, leaderboards, badges, and streaks—often fails because it relies on extrinsic motivators that can actually reduce intrinsic motivation. A famous Stanford study from the 1970s showed that children promised a reward for drawing spent significantly less time drawing later than those who weren't. To truly make a product feel like a game, you must focus on game design, which is about making the product intrinsically rewarding. I look at five core areas: goals, emotions, toys, controls, and flow. A "toy" is something you play with, whereas a "game" is something you play. The best products incorporate "toys"—features that are fun to poke and explore, like our time auto-completer in Superhuman. When you build these "squishy affordances" that react in an analog, playful way, you create moments of delight that keep users engaged without needing artificial rewards.
> A: 游戏化——使用积分、排行榜、徽章和连续记录——常常失败，因为它依赖外部激励，实际上会降低内在动机。斯坦福大学在 1970 年代的一项著名研究表明，承诺给予奖励以绘画的孩子，后来花在绘画上的时间明显少于那些没有奖励的孩子。要真正让产品感觉像个游戏，必须专注于游戏设计，它关乎让产品具有内在的奖励。我关注五个核心领域：目标、情感、玩具、控制和流畅度。“玩具” 是你玩的东西，而 “游戏” 是你参与的东西。最好的产品结合了 “玩具”——那些有趣的特性，像我们在 Superhuman 中的时间自动完成器，值得去戳和探索。当你构建这些以类似模拟的、有趣的方式反应的 “软性容许”，你创造了让用户愉悦的时刻，使他们在不需要人造奖励的情况下保持参与。

A: Convincing people is a puzzle that requires different narratives for different audiences. For investors, I leaned on the sheer size of the market—three billion hours a day spent on email—and the fact that the existing solutions were one-size-fits-all. I pitched it as the biggest market they would see all year. For co-founders, the pitch was about momentum. I didn't just talk; I showed. I bought the domain name, hired a top-tier design agency, produced high-fidelity concepts, and raised a significant first check. By the time I approached potential co-founders, they could see the train was leaving the station. I created a narrative that this was an unassailable, 100-year brand, not just another B2B SaaS tool. It was about proving that we were serious, moving fast, and building something fundamentally different.
> A: 说服人们是一道难题，需要为不同的受众提供不同的叙述。对于投资者，我强调了市场的巨大规模——每人每天花费三十亿小时在电子邮件上——以及现有解决方案是一刀切的事实。我将其推销为他们今年所看到的最大市场。对于联合创始人，推销的重点是势头。我不只是谈论；我展示。我购买了域名，雇佣了顶级设计公司，制作了高保真的概念，并且筹集了第一笔可观的资金。当我接触潜在的联合创始人时，他们能够看到火车正要离开车站。我创造了一个叙述，说明这是一个不可动摇的、百年品牌，而不仅仅是另一个 B2B SaaS 工具。重点在于证明我们是认真的，快速推进，并建立一些根本不同的东西。

**Q: You chose a very high-touch, manual onboarding process for Superhuman. What was the motivation behind this, and how did it impact your product development?**
> **Q: 你为 Superhuman 选择了一个非常高接触、手动的入职流程。这个决定的动机是什么，它对你的产品开发产生了怎样的影响？**

A: I realized early on that getting 10,000 signups is easy, but getting them to stick around and advocate for you is hard. I wanted to control the "blast radius" of our growth to ensure every user became a net promoter. I insisted on onboarding only five people a week, personally handling the calls, and watching them use the product for an hour or two. This allowed me to see every single friction point and bug that a standard analytics dashboard would miss. It wasn't just about customer success; it was about maniacal control over the product experience. We didn't turn the spigot on until we were certain that every user was deliriously happy. This process created a "cult" following and gave us the highest-quality feedback loop I’ve ever experienced.
> A: 我很早就意识到，获取 10,000 个注册很简单，但让他们留下来并为你辩护却很难。我想控制我们增长的 “爆炸半径”，以确保每个用户都成为一个净推荐者。我坚持每周只入职五个人，亲自处理电话，并观察他们使用产品一到两个小时。这让我能够看到每一个摩擦点和一个标准分析仪表板会遗漏的错误。这不仅仅是关于客户成功；而是对产品体验进行了病态的控制。在我们确信每个用户都极其满意之前，我们不会打开水龙头。这个过程创造了一种 “教派” 追随，并给了我们我所经历过的最高质量的反馈循环。

**Q: Can you explain the "product-market fit engine" you developed and how it helps founders measure and improve their fit systematically?**
> **Q: 你能解释一下你开发的 “产品市场契合引擎” 以及它如何帮助创始人系统地衡量和改善契合度吗？**

A: The engine is based on Sean Ellis’s question: "How would you feel if you could no longer use the product?" If more than 40% of your users answer "very disappointed," you have product-market fit. To operationalize this, I added three questions: "Who do you think the product is best for?", "What is the main benefit you get?", and "How can we improve?". The key insight is that you can change the market as easily as the product. By segmenting your users to find the group that is already "very disappointed" if they lose you, you can identify your core persona. Then, you ignore the feedback from users who don't care about your product's "main benefit." Instead, you focus on the "somewhat disappointed" users who *do* value that benefit but are held back by a few specific issues. You fix those, and your score climbs.
> A: 这个引擎基于肖恩·埃利斯的问题：“如果你无法再使用这个产品，你会有什么感觉？” 如果超过 40% 的用户回答 “非常失望”，那么你就有了产品市场契合度。为了将其操作化，我新增了三个问题：“你认为这个产品最适合谁？”，“你获得的主要好处是什么？” 和 “我们可以如何改进？” 关键的洞察是，你可以像改变产品一样轻松地改变市场。通过对用户进行细分，找到那些如果失去你就已经 “非常失望” 的用户群体，你可以识别你的核心用户画像。然后，忽略那些不在意你产品 “主要好处” 的用户反馈。而是专注于那些虽然重视该好处但被几个特定问题束缚住的 “有些失望” 的用户。解决这些问题后，你的得分就会上升。

**Q: How should a founder balance the need to innovate with the need to fix the "boring" bugs and complaints from users?**
> **Q: 创始人应该如何平衡创新的需求与解决用户的 “无聊” 错误和投诉的需求？**

A: I recommend a 50/50 split for every planning cycle. Half of your energy should go into building more of what your happiest users already love—the core value proposition—to keep the innovation moving. The other half should be spent systematically working down the boring list of complaints. If you only focus on innovation, someone else will out-execute you on the basics. If you only focus on complaints, you aren't growing the size of your "very disappointed" (i.e., loyal) user base. You have to do both to maintain product-market fit as you scale into adjacent personas.
> A: 我建议在每个规划周期中保持 50/50 的分配。你的一半精力应该投入到构建你最快乐的用户已经热爱的更多内容中——核心价值主张——以保持创新的进行。另一半应该系统地处理无聊的投诉清单。如果你只关注创新，其他人在基础知识上会超越你。如果你只关注投诉，你的 “非常失望”（即忠实）用户基础的规模不会增长。为了在进入相邻用户时保持产品市场契合度，你必须同时做到这两点。

**Q: What is the most important advice you would give to your younger self starting your first company, Rapportive?**
> **Q: 你给年轻时刚开始你的第一家公司 Rapportive 的自己最重要的建议是什么？**

A: First, have a crystal-clear strategy and stick to it. At Rapportive, I was lucky, but I lacked a company-level thesis. Second, be first-principled about your company's design. You don't have to follow the standard playbook—you can choose not to hire for a long time or to avoid freemium models if they don't fit your product. Third, keep increasing your prices. We waited too long to adjust our pricing, and I believe we left significant ARR on the table. Finally, remember that you have the "right not to serve." If a user doesn't fit your core persona, it is perfectly fine to say no. It protects your brand and allows you to focus on the people who truly love what you've built.
> A: 首先，要有清晰的战略，并坚持下去。在 Rapportive，我很幸运，但我缺乏公司层面的论点。其次，要从第一原则出发考虑你公司的设计。你不必遵循标准的手册——如果不适合你的产品，你可以选择不招聘很长时间或避免免费增值模型。第三，持续提高价格。我们等待太久才能调整定价，我相信我们遗留了显著的年度经常性收入。最后，记住你有 “不服务” 的 “权利”。如果某个用户不符合你的核心用户画像，拒绝他们是完全可以的。这保护了你的品牌，并让你专注于真正热爱你所建立之物的人们。

## Strategic Pricing and the Prosumerization of Enterprise Software
> 策略定价与企业软件的用户生产化

Long-term product success relies on a clear, unassailable strategy that accounts for competition and market dynamics. Pricing should reflect the value provided to "prosumers"—power users who possess economic agency but lack time. By charging premium prices, products can align with the user's ego and professional identity. A deliberate, long-term build process acts as a competitive moat, discouraging competitors who are unwilling to invest the necessary time and capital to achieve similar quality. This strategy focuses on building a 100-year brand rather than chasing short-term growth.
> 长期产品成功依赖于一个明确的、无懈可击的战略，考虑到竞争和市场动态。定价应反映提供给 “用户生产者” 的价值——具有经济权力但缺乏时间的核心用户。通过收取高价，产品可以与用户的自我形象和专业身份保持一致。一个深思熟虑的、长期的构建过程作为竞争护城河，劝阻那些不愿投资必要时间和资本以达到类似质量的竞争对手。这一战略侧重于建立一个百年品牌，而非追逐短期增长。

> 实施科学引擎以实现产品市场契合

Product-market fit can be measured and optimized using a systematic engine based on the question: "How would you feel if you could no longer use the product?" By segmenting users into those who are "very disappointed" and those who are not, teams can focus on the right personas and ignore feedback that does not align with the product's core value. A balanced roadmap—allocating half of the effort to enhancing core benefits and half to fixing user complaints—consistently drives the percentage of "very disappointed" users above the 40% benchmark, creating a repeatable process for growth.
> 产品市场契合可以通过基于问题的系统引擎进行测量和优化：“如果您无法再使用该产品，您会有什么感觉？” 通过将用户分为 “非常失望” 和不失望的人，团队可以专注于正确的人物角色，并忽略与产品核心价值不一致的反馈。一个均衡的路线图——将一半的精力投入到增强核心利益上，将另一半投入到解决用户投诉上——持续推动 “非常失望” 用户的百分比超过 40% 的基准，创造出一个可重复的增长过程。
