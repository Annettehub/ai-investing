# Summary

The global economy is undergoing a fundamental shift as AI, robotics, and energy infrastructure create unprecedented capital demands, necessitating a convergence between finance and technology. Marc Rowan, co-founder of Apollo, explains how the firm has evolved from a distressed asset manager into a trillion-dollar institution providing essential retirement income and financing for the modern industrial renaissance. As public markets become increasingly concentrated in a handful of tech stocks, private markets offer critical diversification and long-term capital solutions for capital-intensive sectors like data centers and defense. Success in this environment requires "clean sheet thinking," where firms prioritize merit-based leadership and adapt to rapid technological change rather than relying on legacy models. Ultimately, the future of finance lies in managing complex credit risks and providing stable, long-term capital to support the next generation of industrial growth.
> 全球经济正经历一场根本性的转变，因为人工智能、机器人技术和能源基础设施产生前所未有的资本需求，这要求金融和科技之间的融合。阿波罗集团的联合创始人马克·罗文解释了该公司如何从一个困境资产管理公司发展为一个提供必要退休收入和现代工业复兴融资的万亿级机构。随着公共市场越来越集中于少数科技股票，私人市场为数据中心和国防等资本密集型行业提供了关键的多元化和长期资本解决方案。在这种环境中取得成功需要 “从零开始的思维”，企业必须优先考虑基于能力的领导并适应快速的技术变化，而不是依赖传统模型。最终，金融的未来在于管理复杂的信用风险并提供稳定的长期资本，以支持下一代工业增长。

# Takeaways

* Private markets are currently the only viable source of diversification, as public equity and fixed income markets have become dangerously concentrated in a handful of tech and financial entities.
  > 私人市场目前是唯一可行的多元化来源，因为公共股票和固定收益市场在少数科技和金融实体中变得危险地集中。
* The massive capital requirements for the next industrial era—spanning AI, robotics, and energy infrastructure—cannot be efficiently financed by venture equity alone and will necessitate a fundamental convergence of technology and private credit.
  > 下一个工业时代的巨大资本需求——涵盖人工智能、机器人和能源基础设施——仅靠风险投资无法高效融资，这将需要技术与私人信贷的基本融合。
* Enterprise software valuations are facing a structural correction because historical pricing models failed to account for the competitive disruption and deflationary pressure introduced by AI.
  > 企业软件估值正在面临结构性调整，因为历史定价模型未能考虑到人工智能带来的竞争性颠覆和通货紧缩压力。
* A significant "hybrid" asset class is emerging for private, investment-grade opportunities that offer superior risk-adjusted returns but currently lack a designated home in traditional institutional portfolio buckets.
  > 一种重要的 “混合” 资产类别正在为私人投资级机会的出现提供卓越的风险调整回报，但目前在传统机构投资组合中缺乏指定的归属。
* Transitioning from a fund-based model to a durable financial institution requires replacing traditional drawdown structures with daily price discovery and standardized data to serve the broader retirement and retail ecosystem.
  > 从基于基金的模型过渡到持久的金融机构需要用日常价格发现和标准化数据替代传统的提款结构，以服务更广泛的退休和零售生态系统。
* AI-driven labor shifts will prioritize the replacement of roles with a definitive "right answer," such as coding and accounting, while roles requiring complex judgment will primarily undergo enhancement.
  > 人工智能驱动的劳动力转型将优先替代具有明确 “正确答案” 的角色，如编程和会计，而需要复杂判断的角色将主要经历增强。
* Hiring based on merit adjusted for "distance traveled" creates a more effective and resilient organizational culture than relying on immutable characteristics or group-based identity.
  > 基于 “已走过的距离” 调整的绩效招聘比依赖不变特征或基于群体身份的招聘创造了更有效和更具韧性的组织文化。
* Long-term organizational success is threatened when leadership mistakes historical processes for the source of their competitive advantage, causing the firm to prioritize the fear of losing over the original drive to win.
  > 当领导层将历史流程误认为是其竞争优势来源时，长期的组织成功会受到威胁，这导致公司优先考虑失去的恐惧而非最初的胜利动力。

**Q: How do you define Apollo’s business model today, given that many still incorrectly label it as just a private equity firm?**
> **Q: 你如何定义今天阿波罗的商业模式，因为很多人错误地将其仅标记为私人股本公司？**

A: Today, Apollo is a trillion-dollar institution operating in two primary businesses: retirement services and asset management. If you look at our assets under management, 80% is in credit, and the vast majority of that is investment-grade. We aren't just a "deal shop"; we serve a fundamental good. We are the largest provider of retirement income in the world, and we are the largest source of financing for the global industrial renaissance taking place in the US, Europe, and Asia. We match the long-term income needs of retirees with the capital needs of large, investment-grade companies building data centers, energy infrastructure, and next-generation manufacturing.
> A: 今天，阿波罗是一个万亿美元的机构，主要经营两个业务：退休服务和资产管理。如果你看看我们的管理资产，80% 在信贷中，而这些资产的绝大多数是投资级的。我们不仅仅是一个 “交易所”；我们服务于基本的好处。我们是世界上最大的退休收入提供者，也是美国、欧洲和亚洲正在进行的全球工业复兴的最大融资来源。我们将退休人员的长期收入需求与为大型投资级公司建设数据中心、能源基础设施和下一代制造所需的资本需求相匹配。

**Q: Why do you argue that private markets are currently the only place for investors to find true diversification?**
> **Q: 你为什么认为私人市场目前是投资者寻求真正多样化的唯一地方？**

A: Right now, 10 stocks in the US account for nearly 50% of the S&P 500, and they are all levered to the same trend. We have effectively levered the country's retirement system to those 10 stocks. The same concentration is happening in the global fixed income market, which is shifting from being dominated by 10 large banks to being dominated by five banks and five tech companies. If you are an investor looking for diversification, you cannot find it in public markets because they are becoming increasingly concentrated. Private markets, however, represent 80% of the economic action in the world—including companies like Anthropic, OpenAI, and SpaceX—yet most investors have zero exposure to them.
> A: 现在，美国的 10 只股票几乎占据了标准普尔 500 指数的 50%，而且它们都铆接在同一趋势上。我们实际上让国家的退休系统依赖于这 10 只股票。全球固定收益市场也出现了同样的集中，正在从被 10 家大型银行主导转变为被 5 家银行和 5 家科技公司主导。如果你是寻找多样化的投资者，你在公共市场上找不到它，因为它们正在变得越来越集中。然而，私人市场代表了世界经济活动的 80%——包括像 Anthropic、OpenAI 和 SpaceX 这样的公司——然而大多数投资者对此却没有任何接触。

**Q: What is your perspective on the impact of AI on the enterprise software sector, specifically regarding private equity returns?**
> **Q: 你对人工智能对企业软件行业的影响，特别是关于私募股权回报有什么看法？**

A: Over the past decade, 30% of the private equity industry has been devoted to enterprise software. I personally expect the returns from that sector to be disastrous. The prices paid for these companies reflected a future that did not have AI in it; now that AI is a competitor, the prospects for selling those assets to the public markets or other buyers are significantly reduced. It doesn't mean these companies are going out of business, but the valuation models were built on a premise that no longer holds.
> A: 在过去的十年中，30% 的私募股权行业被用于企业软件。我个人预计该领域的回报将会灾难性。对这些公司的收购价格反映了一个没有人工智能的未来；而现在人工智能已成为竞争者，向公共市场或其他买家出售这些资产的前景大大减少。这并不意味着这些公司会倒闭，但估值模型是建立在一个不再成立的前提上的。

**Q: You have been very vocal about moral leadership, specifically regarding university management and corporate values. Why did you choose to take such a public stance?**
> **Q: 你对道德领导非常直言不讳，特别是关于大学管理和企业价值观。你为什么选择采取如此公开的立场？**

A: I am a passionate person, and the situation at my alma mater struck me as incredibly unfair and ill-advised. It wasn't about free speech; it was about "preferred speech" and a failure to understand the role of a university in society. I believe in the principle of "do right over easy." It was easy to say nothing or to sign up for popular metrics like those regarding carbon or DEI, but it was harder to stand for meritocracy and moral clarity. We hire based on merit, adjusted for "distance traveled"—meaning we look for individuals who have overcome obstacles—rather than immutable characteristics. I would do it all again because I believe we should be the same person in Texas as we are in California.
> A: 我是一位充满激情的人，我 alma mater 的情况让我感到非常不公平和不明智。这不是关于言论自由，而是关于 “优先言论” 和未能理解大学在社会中的角色。我相信 “做正确的事比简单的事更重要” 的原则。保持沉默或签署有关碳或 DEI 等内容的流行指标是很简单的，但坚持功绩和道德清晰度则更为艰难。我们根据功绩来招聘，调整 “旅行距离”——这意味着我们关注那些克服障碍的个人——而不是不可变的特征。我会再次这样做，因为我相信我们在德克萨斯州应该跟在加州时保持同样的人。

**Q: How do you maintain an entrepreneurial, "play to win" culture as the firm grows to thousands of employees?**
> **Q: 随着公司员工规模达到数千人，你如何保持一种企业家精神的 “拼搏取胜” 文化？**

A: We have spent the last year being extremely deliberate about defining what makes Apollo, Apollo. We have a "wall of shame" where every senior professional acknowledges they have lost money for the firm; if you haven't lost money, you aren't doing enough or taking enough risk. We don't fire people for making a bad decision; we fire them for not owning it or fixing it quickly. We emphasize "clean sheet thinking" and informality. As a founder, I know I am only right about 60% of the time, so we foster an environment of intellectual insubordination where the right answer wins, regardless of who proposes it.
> A: 我们花了一年时间非常明确地定义什么造就了阿波罗。我们有一个 “羞耻墙”，每位高级专业人士都承认他们为公司亏损；如果你没有亏损，那就意味着你没做够，或者没有承担足够的风险。我们不会因为决策失误而解雇员工；我们会因为他们不承担责任或没有快速修正问题而解雇他们。我们强调 “清白的思维” 和非正式性。作为创始人，我知道我只有大约 60% 的时间是对的，所以我们营造一种智力不服从的环境，正确答案无论由谁提出都能赢。

**Q: What opportunities do you see at the intersection of Apollo’s financial scale and the Andreessen Horowitz growth ecosystem?**
> **Q: 你在阿波罗的金融规模与 Andreessen Horowitz 成长生态系统交汇处看到了什么机会？**

A: We are entering a period where the tech ecosystem is becoming capital-intensive on an unimaginable scale. The amount of money needed for data centers, chips, robotics, and defense is massive. That cannot be financed entirely with venture equity because it isn't efficient. We are currently parceling out risks: the venture side handles the fundamental business underwrite, while the infrastructure side—things with hard asset value—is being offloaded into credit markets at the appropriate rate of return. We are moving from a world where tech and finance were separate to one where they must converge to fund the next industrial era.
> A: 我们正进入一个技术生态系统变得资本密集到不可想象规模的时期。用于数据中心、芯片、机器人和国防所需的资金量巨大。这不能完全通过风险投资来融资，因为这并不高效。我们目前正在分散风险：风险投资部门处理基本的商业承保，而基础设施部分——具有硬资产价值的东西——则被以适当的回报率转移到信贷市场。我们正在从技术与金融分离的世界走向一个必须融合以资助下一个工业时代的世界。

**Q: What are the most valuable lessons you learned from Michael Milken during your time at Drexel?**
