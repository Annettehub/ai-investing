Summary
Building high-performing sales organizations requires prioritizing "hunters" who can generate new pipeline rather than relying on order-takers from established monopolies. Effective sales leaders must enforce rigorous performance management, including weekly one-on-ones and consistent field presence, while avoiding "socialist" compensation models where top performers are not adequately rewarded. In the current AI-driven landscape, quota setting should remain data-driven, focusing on productivity per representative rather than arbitrary growth targets. While frontier companies like Anthropic inflate compensation packages, sustainable growth relies on meritocracy, clear accountability, and a focus on booked annual contracts over monthly recurring revenue. Ultimately, the most successful sales teams are built on a foundation of grit, continuous development, and the willingness to make difficult personnel decisions quickly to maintain a high-performance culture.
建立高效能销售组织需要优先考虑能够生成新客户资源的 “猎人”，而不是依赖于来自既定垄断的接单者。有效的销售领导者必须执行严格的绩效管理，包括每周的一对一会议和持续的现场参与，同时避免 “社会主义” 的薪酬模式，在这种模式下，顶尖表现者得不到充分奖励。在当前由人工智能驱动的环境中，配额设定应保持数据驱动，关注每位代表的生产力，而不是任意的增长目标。当像 Anthropic 这样的前沿公司抬高薪酬待遇时，可持续增长依赖于精英主义、明确的问责制，以及关注已签署年度合同而非每月经常性收入。最终，最成功的销售团队是建立在韧性、持续发展和快速做出艰难人事决策以维持高绩效文化的基础之上的。
Takeaways
Hiring from monopoly-dominant companies like Salesforce or ServiceNow often results in poor pipeline generation skills, whereas candidates from lesser-known, tier-three brands demonstrate superior grit and the ability to win with inferior products.
从垄断主导公司的招聘，如 Salesforce 或 ServiceNow，往往导致管道生成技能不足，而来自不太知名的三级品牌的候选人展现出更强的韧性和用劣质产品取得成功的能力。
Setting quotas based on empirical market data rather than company hype is critical; if sales representatives consistently exceed $3-4 million in productivity, the organization is likely under-hiring and missing significant market opportunity.
基于实证市场数据而非公司炒作设定配额至关重要；如果销售代表的生产力持续超过 300 万到 400 万美元，那么该组织可能存在招聘不足，错失了重大市场机会。
Maintaining a high-performance sales culture requires the systematic, quarterly removal of the bottom 10% of the sales force, a practice that increases morale among top performers by reinforcing a meritocratic environment.
维持高绩效的销售文化需要系统性地每季度清除销售队伍中表现最差的 10%，这一做法通过强化优胜劣汰的环境来提高顶尖表现者的士气。
The traditional go-to-market strategy of scaling North America to $100 million before expanding internationally is obsolete in the current hyper-competitive environment, necessitating simultaneous global market entry to capture share.
在当前高度竞争的环境中，传统的市场进入策略，即先在北美扩展到 1 亿美元再进行国际扩张，已过时，必须同时进入全球市场以获取市场份额。
Relying on forward-deployed engineers to drive enterprise sales is often a proxy for an incomplete core product, leading to significant technical debt and long-term support liabilities.
依赖前置工程师推动企业销售通常是对核心产品不完整的替代，导致显著的技术债务和长期支持责任。
The current trend of offering multi-million dollar compensation packages to sales leaders is economically unsustainable and creates a market bubble that will eventually necessitate a structural correction.
当前向销售领导提供数百万美元薪酬套餐的趋势在经济上是不可持续的，并形成了一个市场泡沫，最终需要进行结构调整。
Despite the proliferation of AI-driven prospecting tools, the fundamental effectiveness of direct human interaction—specifically cold calling and phone-based relationship building—remains unmatched in high-stakes enterprise sales.
尽管 AI 驱动的潜在客户开发工具普遍存在，但直接人际互动的基本效用——特别是冷电话和基于电话的关系建设——在高风险企业销售中仍然无可匹敌。
Implementing windfall clauses in compensation plans is a necessary safeguard for early-stage companies to prevent disproportionate commission payouts on outlier, multi-million dollar contracts.
在薪酬计划中实施突发条款是早期公司防止异常、多百万美元合同上不成比例佣金支付的必要保障。
Customer success functions must evolve from providing "free professional services" to leveraging usage-based intelligence and automated analytics to predict churn and drive product consumption.
客户成功职能必须从提供 “免费专业服务” 演变为利用基于使用的智能和自动化分析来预测客户流失并推动产品消费。
Q & A
Q: How can a founder distinguish between a "hunter" and an "order taker" when hiring from companies with famous logos?
Q: 创始人在从拥有知名标志的公司招聘时，如何区分 “猎人” 和 “接单者”？
A: The simplest way to verify a candidate's skill is to ask for specific examples of them opening two to three new logos in the last 24 months. Follow this up with deep, granular questions about who their champion was and who the economic buyer was. If they start faltering or cannot articulate the specifics of the deal, they are likely lying about their role in the win. We generally avoid hiring from companies with monopolies like Salesforce or ServiceNow, where almost every prospect is already a customer. Instead, we look for people who have succeeded at "tier-three" companies with inferior products; if they can win there, they have true pipeline generation skills.
A: 验证候选人技能最简单的方法是要求他们提供在过去 24 个月中开拓两到三个新客户的具体实例。接下来，深入询问他们的支持者是谁，经济购买者又是谁。如果他们开始犹豫或无法清楚地表达交易的细节，他们很可能在撒谎关于自己在这个胜利中的角色。我们通常避免从像 Salesforce 或 ServiceNow 这样的垄断企业招聘，因为几乎每个潜在客户都是现有客户。相反，我们寻找那些在产品较差的 “三线” 公司中取得成功的人；如果他们能在那里赢得胜利，他们就具有真正的管道生成能力。

Q: How should a founder handle underperforming senior talent without letting it drag down the organization?
Q: 创始人应该如何处理表现不佳的高级人才，而不让其拖累整个组织？
A: You have to make hard decisions quickly. I learned from Frank Slootman that if you have doubt, there is no doubt. When you let underperformance linger, it creates rot and demoralizes your A-players, who see that you aren't holding everyone to the same standard. When you do let someone go, be kind but decisive—get in and out quickly, provide the HR paperwork, and don't be an asshole about it. You don't need to trash them in the market, but you must remove them to maintain a performance-based culture.
A: 你必须迅速做出艰难的决定。我从 Frank Slootman 那里学到，如果你有疑虑，那就没什么好疑虑的。当你让表现不佳持续下去，它会造成腐败，并使你的 A 类人才士气低落，他们会看到你没有对每个人都持同样的标准。当你真的让某人离开时，要友善但果断——迅速进出，提供人力资源文书工作，不要在此事上显得无礼。你不需要在市场上抨击他们，但你必须将他们解除职务以维持以绩效为基础的文化。

Q: How do you structure quota setting and compensation when deals can vary wildly in size?
Q: 在交易大小可能差异巨大时，如何构建配额设定和补偿？
A: We implement "windfall clauses" in our compensation plans. If a rep happens to land a massive, outlier deal—say,  4 or  2 million, which is a great outcome, but it prevents the company from overpaying for a single event. I invoked this clause five times while at Snowflake.
A: 我们在补偿计划中实施 “意外条款”。如果某个销售代表恰巧获得一个巨大的，超出常规的交易——比如 2000 万美元——我们并不希望支付他们 400 万或 500 万美元的佣金。该条款允许公司审查特定的交易并重设补偿。你可能仍然会支付他们 200 万美元，这也是一个很好的结果，但它防止公司对单一事件过度支付。我在 Snowflake 时调用过这个条款五次。

Q: How can a smaller company compete for sales talent against giants like Anthropic or OpenAI that are offering $10M+ compensation packages?
Q: 小公司如何与像 Anthropic 或 OpenAI 这样提供 1000 万美元以上薪酬包的巨头竞争销售人才？
A: You cannot compete on raw cash, so you have to sell them on the environment. At those massive AI companies, sales reps are often just "passengers" in a group-quota environment where meritocracy is diluted. If you are a high-performing salesperson, you want to be at a place that actually values your craft, teaches you how to sell, and rewards you based on your individual performance. If a candidate only cares about the massive package and doesn't care about the quality of the sales organization they are joining, they probably aren't the type of person I want in my company anyway.
A: 你无法在现金上竞争，因此你必须向他们推销环境。在这些庞大的 AI 公司中，销售代表往往只是 “乘客”，在一个团队配额的环境中，功绩被稀释。如果你是一个高绩效的销售人员，你希望处在一个实际重视你的技能、教你如何销售并根据你的个人表现来奖励你的地方。如果一个候选人只关心巨大的薪酬包，而不关心他们加入的销售组织的质量，那么他们可能根本不是我想要在公司里的人。

Q: What does a high-value, effective one-on-one meeting actually look like?
Q: 高价值、有效的一对一会议实际上是什么样的？
A: A one-on-one is not a "hey, how are things?" chat. It is a rigorous, weekly inspection of the business. As a manager, you should use AI to review their calendar and forecast before the meeting so you arrive prepared. Use that time to ask hard, specific questions about their deals, their champion, and their progress. If you aren't asking tough questions, you aren't holding them accountable. Consistency is the key; if you only show up once a month, they will realize you aren't actually inspecting their work.
A: 一对一会议不是 “嘿，最近怎么样？” 的闲聊。它是对业务的严格、每周的检查。作为经理，你应该利用人工智能在会议前审查他们的日历和预测，以便做好准备。利用这个时间问他们关于交易、支持者和进展的艰难、具体的问题。如果你不问艰难的问题，你就没有对他们进行问责。一致性是关键；如果你一个月只出现一次，他们会意识到你并没有真正检查他们的工作。

Q: Is it possible to scale from 100 to 300 sales reps in a single year without sacrificing quality?
Q: 在不牺牲质量的前提下，是否有可能在一年内将销售代表从 100 人扩大到 300 人？
A: It is possible, but you have to break the traditional rules. Normally, you want a manager to have no more than six reps, with at least three being productive. When you scale that fast, you end up with managers who have six reps who have all been there for less than 90 days. This breaks your enablement and territory structures. You can only pull this off if the market is massive and your product is something everyone is desperate to buy.
A: 这是可能的，但你必须打破传统规则。通常来说，你希望一个经理最多有六个代表，至少有三个是有生产力的。当你如此快速扩张时，最终你会得到那些已工作不到 90 天的六个代表的经理。这会破坏你的支持和领土结构。只有在市场庞大，且你的产品是每个人都急于购买的情况下，你才能做到这一点。

Q: What is the most controversial belief you have about building enterprise sales organizations?
Q: 你对构建企业销售组织最有争议的看法是什么？
A: The cost is the most controversial part. When I tell a CEO that for every five reps they need a manager, and for every four managers they need a second-line manager, they realize how expensive it is. Then, you tell them that it takes six months to get any productivity out of these hires, and you have to account for 25% annual attrition. Founders often look at those numbers and get sticker shock, but that is the reality of building a healthy, sustainable enterprise sales engine.
A: 成本是最具争议的部分。当我告诉一个 CEO 每五个代表需要一个经理，每四个经理需要一个第二线经理时，他们意识到这有多昂贵。然后，你告诉他们，从这些新雇员那里获得任何生产力需要六个月的时间，并且你必须考虑到 25% 的年度流失率。创始人通常会看这些数字而感到震惊，但这就是构建健康、可持续的企业销售引擎的现实。

Q: How do you handle the "SDR is dead" narrative, and are you moving toward a model of fewer, more expensive "full-stack" reps?
Q: 你如何处理 “SDR 已死” 的说法，是否朝着更少而更昂贵的 “全栈” 代表模型转变？
A: I don't buy that the SDR role is dead. As long as you are selling something critical to a business, you have to build a relationship with a human being. CIOs are getting inundated with AI-generated emails, and they are becoming easy to ignore. The function of picking up the phone, leaving a voicemail, and following up is still the most effective way to break through the noise. My reps are not allowed to just send notes; they have to make the phone call.
A: 我不认为 SDR 角色已经消亡。只要你在销售对业务至关重要的东西，你就必须与人建立关系。CIO 们受到大量 AI 生成电子邮件的轰炸，变得越来越容易被忽视。拨打电话、留言并进行跟进仍然是打破噪音的最有效方式。我的销售代表不允许仅仅发送备注；他们必须打电话。

Q: How should customer success evolve in an AI-driven, consumption-based model?
Q: 客户成功在基于消费的、以人工智能驱动的模型中应该如何发展？
A: Customer success used to be a nebulous role that often functioned as "free professional services." That needs to end. In a consumption model, you have access to massive amounts of usage data. Customer success should be highly analytical and automated. AI can now suggest how to interact with a customer, identify when they are at risk of churning, and guide the relationship based on actual metrics rather than just "checking in."
A: 客户成功曾经是一个模糊的角色，通常充当 “免费专业服务”。这需要结束。在消费模型中，你可以访问大量的使用数据。客户成功应该是高度分析和自动化的。AI 现在可以建议如何与客户互动，识别他们何时面临流失风险，并根据实际指标而非仅仅 “check in” 来指导关系。

Q: How do you identify a "killer" CEO when you are deciding which companies to work with?
Q: 在决定与哪些公司合作时，如何识别 “优秀” 的 CEO？
A: I look for two things: the CEO's intensity and the product's quality. I need a CEO who is in the weeds—the ones who reach out to me at 10:00 PM or 6:00 AM because they are obsessed with the mission. If I suggest a series of actions and they hesitate or push back without a data-driven reason, it’s not going to work. And ultimately, if the product isn't world-class, even the best sales organization in the world will lose. I have had plenty of misses, and they always come down to a lack of product-market fit or a CEO who wasn't a true killer.
A: 我寻找两个方面：CEO 的激情和产品的质量。我需要一个深入参与细节的 CEO——那些在晚上 10:00 或早上 6:00 联系我，因为他们对使命充满热情的人。如果我建议一系列行动，他们犹豫或没有依据的数据原因反对，这就行不通。最终，如果产品不是世界级的，即使是世界上最好的销售组织也会失败。我经历了很多失败，它们总是归结为缺乏产品市场适配或 CEO 不够出色。

ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)

A key financial metric used by subscription-based companies to measure the annualized revenue generated from their customer base. It is a primary indicator of a company's long-term health and growth, often used to determine valuation.

ARR (年度经常性收入)

一个关键的财务指标，用于基于订阅的公司衡量其客户基础所产生的年度化收入。它是公司长期健康和增长的主要指标，常用于确定估值。

MEDIC

A popular sales qualification framework that helps teams assess the viability of a deal by evaluating six key areas: Metrics, Economic buyer, Decision criteria, Decision process, Identify pain, and Champion. It is used to ensure that sales efforts are focused on opportunities that are likely to close.

MEDIC

一个流行的销售资格框架，通过评估六个关键领域来帮助团队评估交易的可行性：指标、经济买家、决策标准、决策流程、识别痛点和推动者。它用于确保销售工作集中在可能成交的机会中。

Consumption Model

A pricing structure where customers are charged based on the amount of a service they actually use, rather than a fixed subscription fee. This model is common in cloud data platforms, as it aligns the customer's cost directly with the value they extract from the product.

消费模型

一种定价结构，客户根据实际使用的服务数量收费，而不是固定的订阅费。这种模型在云数据平台中很常见，因为它将客户的成本直接与他们从产品中获得的价值对齐。

Forward Deployed Engineer

A technical role where engineers work directly with customers to implement, customize, or troubleshoot a product in the field. The transcript notes that while these roles can be valuable for driving product adoption, they are sometimes viewed as a form of professional services that can create technical debt.

前线工程师

一个技术角色，工程师直接与客户合作，在现场实施、定制或故障排除产品。文稿指出，尽管这些角色对推动产品采用很有价值，但有时被视为一种专业服务，可能会产生技术债务。

OTE (On-Target Earnings)

The total compensation a salesperson is expected to earn if they achieve 100% of their assigned sales quota. It typically includes a fixed base salary combined with variable commission payments.

OTE (目标收益)

销售人员在达到 100% 分配销售配额时预期获得的总薪酬。它通常包括固定的基础工资以及可变的佣金支付。

Windfall Clause

A specific provision in a sales compensation plan that allows a company to review or cap commissions for unusually large or unexpected deals. This protects the company from paying out massive, disproportionate bonuses on singular contracts that may not reflect standard sales effort.

意外收益条款

销售薪酬计划中的一个特定条款，允许公司审核或限制对异常大或意外交易的佣金。这保护公司免于在可能不反映标准销售努力的单一合同上支付巨额、不成比例的奖金。

Product-Led Growth (PLG)

A business strategy where the product itself is the primary driver of customer acquisition and expansion. Instead of relying solely on a sales team to convince customers, the company allows users to experience the product's value directly, often through self-serve or trial models.

产品主导增长 (PLG)

一种商业策略，产品本身是客户获取和扩展的主要驱动力。公司不仅依赖销售团队说服客户，而是让用户直接体验产品的价值，通常通过自助服务或试用模型。

New Logo

A term used in sales to refer to the acquisition of a brand-new customer account. Experienced sales leaders often prioritize hiring individuals with a proven track record of bringing in new logos, rather than those who only manage existing relationships.

新客户

在销售中用来指代获得全新客户账户的术语。有经验的销售领导者通常优先招聘有成功记录的人，他们能够带来新客户，而不是仅仅管理现有关系的人。

Snowflake

A major cloud-based data platform company that gained prominence for its consumption-based pricing model. It is frequently referenced in the transcript as a benchmark for scaling a sales organization from zero to billions in revenue.

Snowflake

一家主要的基于云的数据平台公司，以其消费型定价模型而闻名。文稿中经常提到它作为将销售组织从零扩展到数十亿收入的基准。

Anthropic

An AI research and safety company that develops advanced large language models. The speakers discuss how the company’s aggressive hiring and high compensation packages are currently setting new, challenging benchmarks for the broader technology sales talent market.

Anthropic

一家人工智能研究与安全公司，开发先进的大型语言模型。发言者讨论了该公司激进的招聘和高额薪酬套餐目前为更广泛的技术销售人才市场设定了新的挑战性基准。

Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

A formal process used by managers to outline specific goals and expectations for an underperforming employee over a set period. The speakers in the transcript express skepticism about the effectiveness of these plans, often viewing them as a formal step toward an employee's eventual departure.
