# Summary

The AI revolution represents a fundamental shift from retrieval-based computing to generative intelligence, establishing a new industrial infrastructure comparable to the Industrial Revolution. NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang characterizes this transformation through the "AI factory," a system that processes energy into tokens of intelligence. This ecosystem functions as a five-layer industrial cake, spanning energy production, hardware, infrastructure, model development, and application layers. By automating routine tasks, AI elevates human roles, allowing professionals to transition from task-based execution to high-level problem-solving and architectural design. Rather than eliminating jobs, this technology democratizes programming and enhances productivity across sectors like healthcare and manufacturing. As global investment in this infrastructure accelerates, the focus shifts toward integrating AI as a tool to solve complex problems, ultimately cocooning the planet in a continuous, real-time layer of generative intelligence.
> 人工智能革命代表了从基于检索的计算到生成智能的根本转变，建立了一个与工业革命相当的新工业基础设施。NVIDIA 首席执行官黄仁勋通过 “人工智能工厂” 来描述这一转变，这是一种将能量处理成智能代币的系统。这个生态系统作为五层工业蛋糕运作，涵盖能源生产、硬件、基础设施、模型开发和应用层。通过自动化常规任务，人工智能提升了人类的角色，使专业人士能从以任务为基础的执行转变为高水平的问题解决和架构设计。与其说是消除工作，这项技术实际上促进了编程的民主化，并提高了医疗保健和制造等领域的生产力。随着全球对这一基础设施投资的加速，焦点转向将人工智能作为解决复杂问题的工具，最终将地球置于一个持续的、实时的生成智能层中。

# Takeaways

* Computing has transitioned from a 60-year-old retrieval-based paradigm—where data is stored and retrieved—to a generative paradigm where intelligence is produced in real-time based on specific context and queries.
  > 计算已从一个 60 年的基于检索的范式转变——在该范式下，数据被存储和检索——到一个生成性范式，在该范式中，智能根据特定的上下文和查询实时产生。
* The industrial investment landscape for AI is structured as a five-layer cake consisting of energy, compute hardware, infrastructure, foundational models, and application-layer solutions.
  > 人工智能的工业投资格局被构建为一个五层蛋糕，包括能源、计算硬件、基础设施、基础模型和应用层解决方案。
* AI’s capability to learn the "language" of any structured domain—including biology, protein folding, and physical laws—extends its utility far beyond text and image generation into the $80 trillion physical industry.
  > 人工智能学习任何结构化领域 “语言” 的能力——包括生物学、蛋白质折叠和物理法则——将其实用性扩展到远超文本和图像生成的 800 万亿美元的实体产业。
* The shift from simple generative AI to agentic systems enables autonomous problem-solving where agents collaborate, utilize digital tools, and eventually control mechanical systems like robotics and self-driving cars.
  > 从简单的生成式人工智能转向智能体系统，使得自主问题解决成为可能，智能体可以协作、利用数字工具，并最终控制像机器人和自动驾驶汽车这样的机械系统。
* AI does not eliminate jobs but rather elevates the craft of professionals by automating routine tasks, allowing workers to transition from executing manual processes to designing complex solutions.
  > 人工智能并不会消除工作，而是通过自动化日常任务提高专业人员的技术水平，使工人能够从执行手动流程转向设计复杂解决方案。
* By enabling natural language as the primary interface for computing, AI effectively eliminates the technology divide that previously restricted programming to a small percentage of the population.
  > 通过将自然语言作为计算的主要界面，人工智能有效地消除了之前将编程限制在少数人群中的技术鸿沟。
* The current AI infrastructure build-out represents a multi-trillion-dollar opportunity, with the energy sector experiencing its most significant growth potential in over a century to support the massive power requirements of AI factories.
  > 当前人工智能基础设施的建设代表了数万亿美元的机会，能源部门在一个多世纪以来经历了其最大的增长潜力，以支持人工智能工厂的巨大电力需求。

# Q & A

**Q: What is an AI factory, and why is it the most important investment for an enterprise in the coming decade?**
> **Q: 什么是人工智能工厂，为什么它是未来十年企业最重要的投资？**

A: An AI factory represents a fundamental shift in computing. For the last 64 years, computers were primarily retrieval-based systems—you wrote a program or recorded data, saved it to a file, and retrieved it later. Generative AI has reinvented the computer to be a production engine. We call these "AI factories" because they take in energy (atoms) and output intelligence (tokens). These tokens can be reformulated into language, math, proteins, physics, or mechanical commands. This is the largest infrastructure build-out in human history because we are moving from a world where we store information to one where we generate intelligence on demand. Any enterprise that invests in this is essentially investing in the ability to produce intelligence as a commodity.
> A: 人工智能工厂代表了计算的根本转变。在过去的 64 年里，计算机主要是基于检索的系统——你编写一个程序或记录数据，将其保存到文件中，然后稍后再检索它。生成性人工智能重新定义了计算机，使其成为生产引擎。我们称这些为 “人工智能工厂”，因为它们输入能量（原子）并输出智慧（代币）。这些代币可以重新构建成语言、数学、蛋白质、物理或者机械指令。这是人类历史上最大的基础设施建设，因为我们正在从一个存储信息的世界转向一个按需生成智慧的世界。任何在这方面投资的企业本质上是在投资于将智慧作为商品生产的能力。

**Q: How should we visualize the industrial layout of the AI ecosystem to understand where the opportunities lie?**
> **Q: 我们应该如何可视化人工智能生态系统的工业布局，以了解机会在哪里？**

A: Think of the AI industry as a five-layer cake. The bottom layer is energy; this is the greatest opportunity for the energy sector in over a century, as sustainable energy, nuclear, wind, and solar will all be funded to power these factories. The second layer consists of chips, computers, and networking hardware. The third layer is physical infrastructure—the land, power, and data center operations, which are currently in scarce supply. The fourth layer is the model layer, where the foundational AI intelligence is built. Finally, the fifth layer is the application layer, which is where startups and enterprises apply this technology to solve problems in finance, legal, logistics, and healthcare. We are currently putting a trillion dollars into this ecosystem, but the ultimate potential is a $20 trillion-a-year industry because the demand for intelligence is effectively limitless.
> A: 将人工智能行业想象成一个五层蛋糕。底层是能源；这是能源行业 100 多年来最大的机遇，因为可持续能源、核能、风能和太阳能都会获得资金来为这些工厂提供动力。第二层是芯片、计算机和网络硬件。第三层是物理基础设施——土地、电力和数据中心运营，目前供应稀缺。第四层是模型层，构建基础的人工智能智慧。最后，第五层是应用层，初创公司和企业在这里应用这些技术来解决金融、法律、物流和医疗保健中的问题。我们目前正在向这个生态系统投入一万亿美元，但最终的潜力是每年 20 万亿美元的产业，因为对智慧的需求实际上是无穷无尽的。

**Q: How do we address the common fear that AI will eliminate jobs, particularly in fields like radiology or software engineering?**
> **Q: 我们如何应对对人工智能将消除工作岗位的普遍担忧，特别是在放射学或软件工程等领域？**

A: That fear stems from a naive confusion between a "task" and a "purpose." People often look at a specific task—like reading a scan or writing code—and assume that if a computer can do that task, the job is dead. Look at radiology: 12 years ago, experts predicted computer vision would wipe out the profession. Instead, computer vision made radiologists more productive, which made hospitals more profitable, which led to more patients being admitted and ultimately more radiologists being hired. The purpose of a radiologist is to work with doctors to diagnose disease, not just to look at a scan. Similarly, software engineering isn't about typing code; it’s about solving problems. AI doesn't eliminate these jobs; it elevates them. It allows a plumber to become a designer or a carpenter to become an architect.
> A: 这种恐惧源于对 “任务” 和 “目的” 的天真混淆。人们常常关注一个具体的任务——比如阅读扫描结果或编写代码——并假设如果计算机能够完成该任务，那么工作就会消失。看看放射学：12 年前，专家预测计算机视觉将会消灭这个职业。相反，计算机视觉提高了放射科医生的生产力，使医院变得更有利润，从而导致更多患者入院，最终雇佣更多的放射科医生。放射科医生的目的是与医生合作诊断疾病，而不仅仅是查看扫描结果。类似地，软件工程并不是单纯地编写代码；它是解决问题。人工智能并不是消灭这些工作，而是提升了这些工作。它使得水管工能够成为设计师，木匠能够成为建筑师。

**Q: Why is the transition from "retrieval" to "generation" the defining characteristic of this AI revolution?**
> **Q: 为什么从 “检索” 到 “生成” 的转变是这一人工智能革命的定义特征？**

A: For decades, our digital world was based on retrieval—you looked up pre-recorded news, pre-recorded music, and pre-recorded videos. In the future, every pixel you see, every word you read, and every video you watch will be originally generated in real-time based on your specific context. We are building a layer of computing that will "cocoon" the earth, generating intelligence 24/7. Just as the invention of the dynamo 300 years ago allowed us to transition from motion to electricity, our machines allow us to transition from electrons to intelligence. We are moving from a world of static data to a world of dynamic, generated intelligence.
> A: 几十年来，我们的数字世界基于检索——你查找预先录制的新闻、预先录制的音乐和预先录制的视频。在未来，你看到的每一个像素、你阅读的每一个字，以及你观看的每一个视频都将根据你特定的上下文实时生成。我们正在构建一层计算，将 “茧” 包裹地球，24 小时生成智慧。就像 300 年前发电机的发明使我们能够从运动转向电能一样，我们的机器使我们能够从电子转向智慧。我们正在从一个静态数据的世界转变为一个动态生成智慧的世界。

**Q: How does AI learn to process complex physical structures like human biology or physics, rather than just text?**
> **Q: 人工智能如何学习处理复杂的物理结构，例如人类生物学或物理，而不仅仅是文本？**

A: From the computer's perspective, it doesn't matter if the input is a word, a protein, a cell, or a car—it is all just tokens. If something has structure and is predictable, we can learn its "language." We have learned the language of human biology, genes, and the physical world just as we learned the language of human speech. Once we represent the world's information in a way the computer can understand, it can reason about it, create a plan, and generate an action. This is why AI is so powerful; it allows us to apply intelligence to the $80 trillion physical world, not just the digital one. If the system is predictable, we can learn the meaning behind it, and that allows us to solve problems that were previously unsolvable.
> A: 从计算机的角度来看，输入是一个单词、一个蛋白质、一个细胞或一辆汽车都无所谓——它们都只是代币。如果某物有结构且是可预测的，我们就可以学习它的 “语言”。我们已经学会了人类生物学、基因和物理世界的语言，就像我们学习人类语言一样。一旦我们以计算机可以理解的方式表示世界的信息，它就可以推理，制定计划并生成行动。这就是人工智能如此强大的原因；它使我们能够将智慧应用于 80 万亿美元的物理世界，而不仅仅是数字世界。如果系统是可预测的，我们就能学习其背后的含义，这允许我们解决以前无法解决的问题。

## The Five-Layer Industrial Framework for AI Investment

The industrial landscape of AI is structured as a five-layer cake, representing a multi-trillion-dollar investment opportunity.
