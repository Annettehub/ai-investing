# Summary

AI represents a fundamental technological shift comparable to the internet or mobile, yet its long-term economic impact remains characterized by radical uncertainty. Benedict Evans, an independent analyst and former A16Z partner, argues that while AI automates specific tasks, it does not necessarily replace entire jobs; instead, it mirrors historical patterns where automation unlocks new, unforeseen roles and increases overall prosperity. Professional services firms remain vital because they solve complex organizational problems that go beyond simple task execution. Furthermore, foundation model companies may face margin compression as their technology becomes a commodity, shifting value toward the application layer where distribution and specific use cases provide a competitive moat. Ultimately, the most effective strategy for individuals is to actively engage with AI tools to understand their practical utility rather than adopting a stance of passive resistance or moral superiority.
> 人工智能代表了一场与互联网或移动设备相 comparable 的基础性技术变革，但其长期经济影响仍然具有 radical uncertainty 的特征。独立分析师、前 A16Z 合伙人本尼迪克特·埃文斯认为，尽管人工智能自动化特定任务，但并不一定会取代整个工作；相反，它反映了历史模式，其中自动化解锁了新的、不可预见的角色，并增加了整体繁荣。专业服务公司仍然至关重要，因为它们解决超越简单任务执行的复杂组织问题。此外，基础模型公司可能面临利润率压缩，因为它们的技术变得商品化，这将价值转向应用层，在这里分发和特定用例提供竞争壁垒。最终，个人最有效的策略是积极使用人工智能工具，以了解其实际效用，而不是采取消极抵抗或道德优越的态度。

# Takeaways

* AI represents a fundamental technological shift comparable to the internet or mobile, currently existing in a nascent "1997 moment" where most applications are unproven and the ultimate winners remain unclear.
  > AI 代表了一场与互联网或移动技术相媲美的基础性技术转变，目前处于初生的 “1997 时刻”，大多数应用尚未得到验证，最终的赢家仍然不明确。
* The "lump of labor" fallacy persists; while automation eliminates specific tasks, it historically triggers a net increase in employment by creating entirely new, previously unimaginable job categories.
  > “劳动总量” 谬论依然存在；虽然自动化消除了特定任务，但历史上它促使了就业的净增加，创造了完全新的、以前无法想象的工作类别。
* Foundation models risk becoming low-margin commodity infrastructure, with the majority of long-term economic value likely accruing to the application layer where companies solve specific, complex business problems.
  > 基础模型有风险成为低利润的商品基础设施，大多数长期经济价值可能会积累在应用层，企业在这里解决特定的复杂商业问题。
* The surge in AI labs investing in professional services highlights that the primary barrier to AI adoption is not the technology itself, but the organizational capacity to redesign internal workflows and manage complex change.
  > AI 实验室在专业服务上的投资激增凸显出，AI 采用的主要障碍不是技术本身，而是组织重新设计内部工作流程和管理复杂变革的能力。
* Distinguishing between a "task" and a "job" is critical; while AI can automate discrete tasks like drafting a slide deck, it cannot easily replace the strategic, political, and customer-centric components that define a professional role.
  > 区分 “任务” 和 “工作” 至关重要；虽然 AI 可以自动化一些离散任务，例如起草幻灯片，但它不能轻易替代定义专业角色的战略、政治和以客户为中心的组成部分。
* As software development becomes increasingly accessible, distribution—rather than technical superiority—is emerging as the most significant competitive moat for companies.
  > 随着软件开发变得愈加可及，分发（而非技术优势）正成为公司最重要的竞争护城河。
* The current lack of a theoretical framework for human intelligence or model performance necessitates a posture of radical uncertainty, as historical precedents show that predicting the trajectory of platform shifts is notoriously unreliable.
  > 目前缺乏人类智能或模型性能的理论框架， necessitates a posture of radical uncertainty，因为历史先例表明，预测平台转变的轨迹极不可靠。
* Technological adoption typically follows a three-stage progression: first, performing existing tasks more efficiently; second, creating new capabilities; and finally, completely redefining the underlying business model.
  > 技术采用通常遵循三个阶段的进程：首先，更高效地执行现有任务；其次，创造新的能力；最后，完全重新定义基础商业模型。

# Q & A

**Q: Why is the current "jobpocalypse" narrative surrounding AI likely incorrect, and how should we view the impact of AI on jobs instead?**
> **Q: 为什么围绕人工智能的当前 “工作末日” 叙述可能是错误的，我们应该如何看待人工智能对工作的影响？**

A: Every time we have a new technology, it automates away a bunch of jobs, but that automation also unlocks a bunch of new jobs that we cannot yet imagine. We have seen this process repeat since 1800. The people predicting that companies will buy ChatGPT and immediately fire all their staff are missing how the world works—enterprise sales cycles are long, and companies don't have people sitting around waiting to build new projects. It takes years for industries to integrate these tools and redesign their workflows. The jobs that go away are often seen retrospectively as "crap jobs," and the new jobs created are generally better, leading to higher overall prosperity.
> A: 每当我们有一个新技术时，它会自动化大量工作，但这种自动化也会释放出许多我们还无法想象的新工作。自 1800 年以来，我们看到了这个过程的反复。那些预测公司会购买 ChatGPT 并立即解雇所有员工的人，忽视了世界的运作方式——企业销售周期很长，公司不会有员工闲着等着去建立新项目。行业需要多年时间来整合这些工具并重新设计工作流程。消失的工作往往在事后被视为 “糟糕的工作”，而新创造的工作通常更好，导致整体繁荣度提高。

**Q: Why are the most advanced AI labs investing so heavily in professional services and consulting firms?**
> **Q: 为什么最先进的人工智能实验室在专业服务和咨询公司上投入如此之多？**

A: Large companies do not have internal teams sitting around with the capacity to build big new projects or redesign their entire internal workflows. When a company wants to implement a major change—like plugging new AI systems into existing vertical and horizontal systems—they don't have the internal headcount to do it. They hire firms like Bain, BCG, or Accenture to walk all over the company, understand the politics, talk to customers, and execute the project. The AI labs are investing in this because the "hard part" of the job isn't just generating a PowerPoint deck or writing a line of code; it's the complex, organizational work of implementation and change management.
> A: 大公司并没有内部团队有能力构建大型新项目或重新设计他们的整个内部工作流程。当一家公司想要实现重大变化——例如将新人工智能系统接入现有的垂直和水平系统时——他们没有足够的内部人力来完成。于是，他们雇佣像贝恩、BCG 或埃森哲这样的公司，深入整个公司，理解公司内部的政治，与客户交谈，并执行项目。人工智能实验室之所以投资于此，是因为工作的 “难点” 不仅仅在于生成一个 PowerPoint 演示文稿或编写一行代码；而在于实施和变更管理的复杂组织工作。

**Q: If we are in a "1997 moment" for AI, where in the value chain will most of the value accrue?**
> **Q: 如果我们认为人工智能正处于 “1997 时刻”，那么大部分价值将在价值链的哪里积累？**

A: Foundation models may end up being undifferentiated commodity infrastructure providers, similar to the mobile telecoms industry. While they require enormous capital and sophistication, they may not have pricing power if they lack network effects and if the market remains competitive. If the chatbot isn't the primary user experience and the value lies in thousands of specific applications, the value will likely accrue further up the stack. Just as developers didn't standardize on AWS because of the infrastructure itself, but because it was where the software lived, the value will likely be captured by the companies building the actual applications that solve specific problems for customers.
> A: 基础模型可能最终成为与移动电信行业类似的同质化商品基础设施提供商。虽然它们需要巨额的资本和复杂性，但如果缺乏网络效应且市场保持竞争，它们可能没有定价能力。如果聊天机器人不是主要的用户体验，价值体现在成千上万的特定应用上，那么价值可能会进一步流向更高的层级。正如开发者并不是因为基础设施本身而标准化 AWS，而是因为软件住在那里，价值很可能由实际构建解决客户特定问题应用的公司来捕获。

**Q: What should people do to be more successful in a future shaped by AI?**
> **Q: 人们应该如何在一个由人工智能塑造的未来中更加成功？**

A: Do not stick your head in the sand or adopt a stance of moral superiority by shouting about how evil AI is. That approach is not helpful. Instead, dive into the technology, submerge yourself in it, and come out understanding what you can actually do with it. You need to internalize how these tools change your field and how you can use them to become a better hire. Even if you are worried about the future, the only way to navigate it is to understand the tools that are driving the change.
> A: 不要把头埋在沙子里，或通过高喊人工智能是多么邪恶而自以为是。那种做法没有帮助。相反，深入了解技术，沉浸其中，出来时理解你能用它做什么。你需要内化这些工具如何改变你的领域，以及你如何可以利用它们成为更好的雇员。即使你对未来感到担忧，导航的唯一方式就是理解推动变革的工具。

**Q: How should we interpret the "anti-AI" sentiment growing in the public sphere?**
> **Q: 我们应该如何解读公众领域日益增长的 “反人工智能” 情绪？**

A: It is a big, fuzzy mass of different concerns, some of which are real and some of which are not. While there are tangible issues—like the planning problems associated with data center water and energy usage or the displacement of specific creative roles—much of the backlash is driven by misinformation. For example, the claim that "Facebook sells your data" is widely believed despite being untrue. We have seen similar backlashes with every major technology shift, including the panic around databases in the 70s. The challenge is to distinguish between legitimate concerns and the "moral panic" that often accompanies new technology.
> A: 这是一团庞大而模糊的不同担忧，有些是实际的，有些则不是。虽然确实存在一些切实的问题——例如与数据中心水和能源使用相关的规划问题，或特定创意角色的取代——但大部分反弹源于错误信息。例如，“Facebook 出售你的数据” 的说法尽管不真实，却广泛被相信。我们在每一次重大技术变革中都看到了类似的反弹，包括 70 年代对数据库的恐慌。挑战在于区分合法的担忧和通常伴随新技术而来的 “道德恐慌”。

**Q: How can we distinguish between a "task" that gets automated and a "job" that remains?**
> **Q: 我们如何区分被自动化的 “任务” 和仍然存在的 “工作”？**

A: Sometimes a task *is* the job, like an elevator attendant, which was easily automated. However, in most professional services, the task (like making a slide deck) is not the job. The job involves understanding customer needs, navigating internal politics, and determining what features to build. AI can generate a crappy version of a McKinsey deck, but that isn't what clients pay McKinsey for. The "hard part" of the job is the synthesis and the human-centric decision-making. We have to be careful not to fall for the "expert systems" fallacy where we break a job into tiny pieces and assume that because 17% of the tasks are automatable, the job is 17% gone. It is much more complicated than that.
> A: 有时一个任务 * 就是 * 工作，比如电梯服务员，这个工作很容易被自动化。然而，在大多数专业服务中，任务（比如制作幻灯片）并不是工作。工作涉及理解客户需求、应对内部政治和确定构建哪些特性。人工智能可以生成一份劣质的 McKinsey 演示文稿，但这并不是客户付费给 McKinsey 的原因。工作的 “难点” 在于综合与以人为中心的决策。我们必须小心不要陷入 “专家系统” 的谬论，我们把一项工作拆解成很小的部分，然后假设因为 17% 的任务可以自动化，这项工作就消失了 17%。事情远比这复杂得多。

## Professional Services and the Nature of Work
> 专业服务与工作的性质

> 人工智能实验室正越来越多地投资于专业服务和前线工程师，因为公司缺乏重新设计工作流程的内部能力。工作的难点很少在于任务本身——例如编写代码或创建幻灯片——而在于理解商业问题、客户需求和组织政治。自动化通常遵循杰文斯悖论，其中提高效率会导致需求增加，而不是工作消失。因此，即使是先进的人工智能公司也在增加员工，因为将新技术整合到企业环境中的复杂性需要大量的人类干预和长期项目管理。

## Distribution Moats and Platform Dynamics
> 分发护城河与平台动态

As software becomes easier to build, distribution is emerging as a critical moat for companies. Incumbents with existing user bases have a significant advantage in deploying AI features, as seen in the browser wars where Microsoft used distribution to gain market share. However, winning the platform layer does not guarantee long-term success if the value resides elsewhere. Current AI strategies, such as those from Google and Meta, focus on spraying adequate AI products across every surface to capture user inertia. This mirrors historical platform shifts where the most successful companies were not necessarily the ones with the best underlying technology, but those with the best distribution.
> 随着软件变得更容易构建，分发逐渐成为公司的一项关键护城河。拥有现有用户基础的产业巨头在部署人工智能功能方面具有显著优势，这在浏览器战争中得到了体现，微软利用分发来获得市场份额。然而，赢得平台层并不保证长期成功，如果价值存在于其他地方。当前的人工智能策略，例如谷歌和 Meta 的策略，专注于在每个表面上喷洒足够的人工智能产品以捕获用户惯性。这反映了历史平台转变的情况，其中最成功的公司不一定是具有最佳基础技术的，而是拥有最佳分发的公司。
