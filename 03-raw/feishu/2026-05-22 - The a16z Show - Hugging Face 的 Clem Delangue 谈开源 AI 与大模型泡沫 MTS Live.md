# Summary

Open-source AI serves as a critical engine for innovation, yet current regulatory trends risk stifling progress by restricting access to powerful models. Rather than imposing blanket restrictions that mirror tying hands to prevent violence, effective oversight should focus on regulating specific bad actors and illegal activities like cyberattacks. While the U.S. historically led the open-source movement, China has emerged as a major contributor, creating a competitive landscape that necessitates increased international collaboration. The current investment surge in closed-source large language models suggests a potential bubble, whereas open-source platforms like Hugging Face provide essential infrastructure for hosting massive datasets and fostering decentralized development. Beyond software, robotics represents the next frontier for AI, where open-source frameworks enable new physical-world applications and empower users to build custom solutions, ultimately creating more resilient and transparent technological ecosystems.
> 开源人工智能作为创新的关键引擎，但目前的监管趋势可能通过限制对强大模型的访问而扼杀进展。有效的监管应专注于监管特定的不法分子和网络攻击等非法活动，而不是实施一刀切的限制，这就像用绳子绑住手以防止暴力。尽管美国历史上引领了开源运动，但中国已成为一个重要贡献者，创造了一个需要加强国际合作的竞争环境。当前对封闭源大型语言模型的投资激增暗示了一种潜在的泡沫，而像 Hugging Face 这样的开源平台则提供了托管大量数据集和促进分散开发的基本基础设施。除了软件，机器人技术代表了人工智能的下一个边界，开源框架使得新的现实世界应用成为可能，并使用户能够构建定制解决方案，最终创造出更具弹性和透明度的技术生态系统。

# Q & A

**Q: Why do you argue that open-sourcing AI models is actually safer than keeping them behind closed APIs?**
> **Q: 为什么你认为开源 AI 模型实际上比将它们隐藏在封闭的 API 后面更安全？**

A: The most significant risk in AI is an asymmetry of capabilities where a few players possess tools that others—specifically the defenders—do not have. If we restrict access, we ensure that bad actors might still find ways to use these capabilities, but the people trying to build protection systems are left without the necessary tools to defend against them. By making models open, we empower the entire community to not only build innovative systems but also to develop the security and protection mechanisms required to make those systems safe. History shows that concerns about "dangerous" models are often overblown; we see this cycle with every release, where initial fear gives way to adaptation and widespread, safe utility.
> A: AI 中最显著的风险是能力的不对称，少数参与者拥有其他人—特别是防御者—没有的工具。如果我们限制访问，我们确保不良行为者可能仍会找到使用这些能力的方法，但试图建立保护系统的人却没有必要的工具来防御他们。通过开放模型，我们赋予整个社区不仅构建创新系统的能力，还开发使这些系统安全所需的安全和保护机制。历史表明，对 “危险” 模型的担忧往往被夸大；我们在每次发布时都能看到这一周期，最初的恐惧让位于适应和广泛的安全效用。

**Q: How do you respond to the argument that we should restrict AI development to prevent potential harm?**
> **Q: 你如何回应我们应该限制 AI 发展以防止潜在伤害的论点？**

A: The idea of restricting AI based on potential risks is like saying, "Some people can punch others, so let’s tie down everybody’s hands." While it is true that some individuals might use their hands to cause harm, the reality is that hands are incredibly useful tools for creating, building, and interacting with the world. You don't solve the problem by disabling everyone; you solve it by untying them and focusing on regulating or fighting the specific bad actors. If hacking creates cybersecurity risks, we make hacking illegal and fight the behavior, but we don't prevent the entire population from having the capability to use computers. Restricting AI only slows down progress and creates massive, dangerous gaps in control and capability.
> A: 基于潜在风险限制 AI 的想法就像在说：“有些人可能会打人，所以让我们绑住每个人的手。” 虽然确实有些个体可能会用手造成伤害，但现实是，手是创造、构建和与世界互动的极其有用的工具。通过使每个人失去能力并不能解决问题；解决问题的方法是解开他们的束缚，专注于监管或打击特定的不良行为者。如果黑客行为造成网络安全风险，我们就让黑客行为变得非法并打击这种行为，但并不是阻止整个社会拥有使用计算机的能力。限制 AI 只会减缓进展，并在控制和能力上造成巨大的危险差距。

**Q: You’ve mentioned that we might be in an "LLM bubble." What do you mean by that, and is the entire AI industry at risk?**
> **Q: 你提到过我们可能处于一个 “LLM 泡沫” 中。你是什么意思？整个 AI 行业是否面临风险？**

A: I don't believe AI as a general field is in a bubble, but there is a specific domain where we are seeing massive over-investment: large language models distributed behind closed APIs. We see companies building these massive, expensive data centers with uncertain long-term margins and unclear "moats" to protect their business models. While there is definitely revenue growth, the sustainability of this specific approach is questionable. We will see how this plays out over the next few months, but the "bubble" is localized to that specific way of deploying and monetizing LLMs, not to the broader potential of AI technology.
> A: 我不认为 AI 作为一个整体领域处于泡沫中，但在一个特定领域，我们确实看到大量的过度投资：分布在封闭 API 后面的特大语言模型。我们看到公司在建设这些庞大且昂贵的数据中心，但长期利润不确定，保护其商业模式的 “护城河” 也不明确。虽然这确实带来了收入增长，但这种特定方式的可持续性是值得怀疑的。我们将在接下来的几个月中观察这一情况的进展，但 “泡沫” 仅限于那种特定的 LLM 部署和获利方式，而不是 AI 技术的更广泛潜力。

**Q: How has the landscape of open-source AI shifted between the U.S. and China in recent years?**
> **Q: 最近几年美国和中国开放源代码 AI 的格局如何变化？**

A: Historically, the U.S. was the powerhouse of open-source, which is what fueled the current AI revolution—after all, the "T" in ChatGPT comes from the Transformer architecture, which was open-sourced by Google. Unfortunately, that trend has shifted, and we’ve seen a move toward closing down models behind proprietary APIs. Conversely, China has moved in the opposite direction and is now the strongest contributor to open-source AI. If you talk to startups or researchers in the U.S. today, they are frequently relying on Chinese open-source models like DeepSeek, Qwen, or Kimi because of their massive contributions to the field.
> A: 历史上，美国是开源的强国，这正推动了当前的 AI 革命——毕竟，ChatGPT 中的 “T” 源自于谷歌开源的 Transformer 架构。不幸的是，这一趋势已经转变，我们看到模型越来越多地被封闭在专有 API 后面。相反，中国则朝着相反的方向发展，现在成为开放源代码 AI 的最强贡献者。如果今天与美国的初创公司或研究人员交谈，他们经常依赖中国的开源模型，如 DeepSeek、Qwen 或 Kimi，因为这些模型对该领域的贡献巨大。

**Q: Why did Hugging Face become the go-to platform for AI developers rather than GitHub?**
> **Q: 为什么 Hugging Face 成为 AI 开发者的首选平台，而不是 GitHub？**

A: While people often call us the "GitHub of AI," the reality is that hosting AI artifacts is fundamentally different from hosting code. GitHub is optimized for text-based code, whereas we deal with a volume of data that is exponentially larger. Just last week, we added two petabytes of data to our platform—the equivalent of 500,000 two-hour movies. We had to build our infrastructure specifically to handle these massive model files, datasets, and the unique lifecycle of AI development. We provide a space for both public and private usage that is structurally tailored to the needs of AI builders, which is why we’ve become the central hub for the community.
> A: 虽然人们常常称我们为 “AI 的 GitHub”，但事实是，托管 AI 文档与托管代码有根本的不同。GitHub 针对文本代码进行了优化，而我们处理的数据量则是指数级增长的。就在上周，我们向平台添加了两 PB 的数据，相当于 50 万部两小时的电影。我们必须专门构建基础设施来处理这些庞大的模型文件、数据集以及 AI 开发的独特生命周期。我们提供一个为 AI 构建者量身定制的公共和私用空间，这就是我们成为社区中心枢纽的原因。

---

**Q: You are currently pushing into the robotics space with projects like LeRobot. Why is open-source critical for the future of physical AI?**
> **Q: 你目前正通过像 LeRobot 这样的项目推动机器人领域的发展。为什么开源对物理 AI 的未来至关重要？**

A: Robotics is the next frontier unlocked by AI, and it moves the technology from a screen into the physical world. We’ve already shipped nearly 10,000 units of our small robot, and we’ve seen over 300 apps created for it by the community. When you put AI on a kitchen table to help with cooking or interact with kids in a way that isn't just staring at a laptop, you realize how empowering it is. Because China is already dominating in robotics, it is vital that we continue to build and innovate in the U.S. using an open-source approach. This allows for rapid iteration and new use cases that simply aren't possible when you keep the hardware and software locked away in a silo.
> A: 机器人技术是 AI 解锁的下一个前沿，它将技术从屏幕转移到物理世界。我们已经发出了近 10,000 个小机器人，并且看到社区为此创造了超过 300 个应用。当你把 AI 放在厨房桌子上帮助烹饪或以不同于盯着笔记本电脑的方式与孩子互动时，你意识到这有多么有力量。由于中国在机器人领域已经占据主导地位，我们必须继续通过开源的方法在美国进行构建和创新。这允许快速迭代和新的用例，这在将硬件和软件锁起来时是根本无法实现的。
