# Summary

The "SaaSpocalypse" narrative—the belief that AI will collapse traditional SaaS business models—lacks empirical support in current corporate spending data. Analysis of $100 billion in annual business expenditures reveals that seat-based contracts remain the dominant payment structure, while token-based pricing represents less than one percent of total spend. Rather than replacing legacy software, AI adoption is currently additive, with firms increasingly deploying multiple models to balance cost and performance. Significant growth is emerging in specialized infrastructure and workflow layers, such as Answer Engine Optimization, which operate independently of frontier model labs. While AI acts as a potent disruptor, business spending patterns indicate that established SaaS providers retain significant stickiness, and the transition toward AI-native workflows is a gradual, nuanced evolution rather than an immediate industry-wide collapse.
> “萨斯启示录” 叙述——即人工智能将破坏传统 SaaS 商业模式的信念——在当前企业支出数据中缺乏实证支持。对 1000 亿美元年商业支出的分析表明，基于席位的合同仍然是主要的付款结构，而基于代币的定价占总支出的比例不足百分之一。在替代传统软件方面，人工智能的采用目前是附加性的，企业越来越多地部署多种模型以平衡成本和性能。在专业基础设施和工作流层中，显著增长正在出现，例如答案引擎优化，这些层独立于前沿模型实验室运行。尽管人工智能作为一种强有力的颠覆者，但商业支出模式表明，已建立的 SaaS 提供商仍然保持重要的粘性，并且向人工智能本土工作流的过渡是一个渐进的、细致的演变，而不是立即的行业崩溃。

# Takeaways

* The widely cited "SaaSpocalypse" narrative—predicting the collapse of traditional SaaS due to AI—is quantitatively unsupported by actual business spending data, which shows continued stickiness for established software platforms.
  > 广泛引用的 “SaaSpocalypse” 叙述——预测传统 SaaS 因 AI 而崩溃——在实际商业支出数据上没有定量支持，这些数据表明成熟软件平台仍然具有持续的粘性。
* Despite industry hype, token-based pricing models account for less than 0.5% of total software spend, while traditional seat-based contracts remain the dominant structure at 65-75%.
  > 尽管行业炒作，基于代币的定价模型占总软件支出的比例不到 0.5%，而传统的按席位计费合同仍然以 65-75% 的比例占据主导结构。
* Businesses are increasingly diversifying their AI infrastructure by deploying multiple models across their organizations rather than consolidating around a single frontier model provider.
  > 企业正在通过在组织内部署多个模型来逐步多样化其 AI 基础设施，而不是围绕单一的前沿模型提供商整合。
* High-intensity AI users are experiencing a 13x increase in token costs, forcing a shift toward routing platforms that allow for model selection based on cost-efficiency and specific task requirements.
  > 高强度的 AI 用户经历了代币成本增加 13 倍，迫使他们转向允许根据成本效益和具体任务要求选择模型的路由平台。
* The AI ecosystem is fostering entirely new software categories, such as Answer Engine Optimization (AEO), which are growing rapidly and independently of the major model labs.
  > AI 生态系统正在催生全新的软件类别，如答案引擎优化（AEO），这些类别正在迅速增长，并且与主要模型实验室独立发展。
* There is a noticeable decoupling of revenue growth from headcount growth in software companies, though it remains early to attribute this shift solely to AI-driven productivity gains.
  > 软件公司的收入增长与员工人数增长之间明显脱钩，尽管将这种转变仅仅归因于 AI 驱动的生产力提升仍然为时尚早。
* The survival of legacy software firms is determined less by direct AI disruption and more by their ability to adapt to evolving customer needs and integrate AI features that provide tangible, measurable value.
  > 传统软件公司的生存与直接的 AI 破坏关系不大，更多取决于其适应不断变化的客户需求的能力，以及整合提供切实、可衡量价值的 AI 功能的能力。

# Q & A

**Q: Is the "SaaSpocalypse"—the theory that AI will destroy traditional SaaS companies—supported by current business spending data?**
> **Q: “SaaSpocalypse”——人工智能将摧毁传统 SaaS 公司的理论——是否有当前商业支出数据的支持？**

A: Quantitatively, the SaaSpocalypse is not supported by actual business behavior. When we look at spend data from 50,000 businesses, we see that neither of its two core predictions is coming true: businesses are not shifting away from traditional SaaS providers, and the way they buy software hasn't meaningfully changed. Seat-based contracts still account for about 65-75% of spend, while flat platform fees make up 20-30%. Even among companies offering token-based pricing, we only see an uptake of about half a percent of total spend.
> A: 从量化的角度来看，SaaSpocalypse 并没有得到实际商业行为的支持。当我们查看 50,000 家企业的支出数据时，我们看到其两个核心预测都没有实现：企业并没有从传统 SaaS 供应商那里转移，购买软件的方式也没有发生显著变化。基于席位的合同仍占支出的大约 65-75%，而固定平台费用则占 20-30%。即使在提供基于代币定价的公司中，我们也仅看到总支出的约千分之五的增长。

---

**Q: Why does the narrative of a "SaaSpocalypse" persist if the data contradicts it?**
> **Q: 如果数据与 “SaaSpocalypse” 的叙述相矛盾，为什么这种叙述依然存在？**

A: A lot of these pronouncements come from pundits and industry observers making projections about how products *might* develop, rather than being informed by what is actually happening in the market. There is a tendency in the tech sector to engage in "TAM-posting"—justifying massive valuations by claiming that AI will replace the entire labor market or disrupt all existing software. It’s a narrative designed for a very small, institutional audience of investors and controllers of capital, rather than a reflection of the reality faced by the average business.
> A: 这些声明很多来自于专家和行业观察者，他们对产品可能如何发展的进行预测，而不是基于市场上实际发生的情况。在科技行业中，有一种 “市场总额 (TAM) 贴文” 的倾向——通过声称人工智能将取代整个劳动市场或干扰所有现有软件来证明巨额估值。这是一种针对极小的机构投资者和资本控制者的叙述，而不是对普通企业所面临现实的反映。

---

**Q: What trends are you observing regarding the adoption of AI models in the workplace?**
> **Q: 你观察到关于 AI 模型在工作场所应用的哪些趋势？**

A: We are seeing a clear shift toward multi-model adoption. Early adopters, who tend to lead the way for the rest of the market, are increasingly using more than one model across their workforce. While OpenAI was the leader for a long time, Anthropic has recently taken the top spot in our Ramp AI Index. The market is not consolidating around one provider; instead, businesses are experimenting with different models to see what fits their specific needs.
> A: 我们看到朝多模型采纳的明显转变。早期采用者通常引领市场，他们在工作队伍中越来越多地使用多于一个模型。虽然 OpenAI 曾长期位居领先，但 Anthropic 最近在我们的 Ramp AI 指数中占据了首位。市场并没有围绕一个供应商进行整合；相反，企业正在尝试不同的模型，以找出最符合他们特定需求的选择。

---

**Q: How are businesses managing the rising costs of AI implementation?**
> **Q: 企业如何管理 AI 实施成本上升的问题？**

A: For high-intensity users, token costs have increased 13x over the last year. While this currently represents a small share of total business spend (roughly 2%), it is an unsustainable trajectory. Consequently, we are seeing firms shift some of their AI spend toward platforms like OpenRouter, which allow them to select between multiple models and take advantage of cheaper or open-source alternatives. I expect this practice of "model routing" to move down the chain to more mainstream firms as they become more cost-conscious.
> A: 对于高强度用户而言，代币成本在过去一年中增加了 13 倍。尽管这目前仅占总商业支出的一个小部分（大约 2%），但这是一条不可持续的轨迹。因此，我们看到公司将部分 AI 支出转向像 OpenRouter 这样的平台，使他们能够在多个模型之间进行选择，并利用更便宜或开源的替代品。我预计这种 “模型路由” 的做法将向更主流的公司扩展，因为它们变得更加注重成本。

---

**Q: Are there new software categories emerging specifically because of the rise of AI?**
> **Q: 有哪些新软件类别是专门因为 AI 的崛起而出现的吗？**

A: Yes, one of the most compelling growth areas is AEO, or "Answer Engine Optimization." Just as SEO emerged to help firms show up in Google search results, AEO is the software that firms use to track their performance within AI models and determine if they are being recommended. This is a new, fast-growing sector that is not dominated by the model companies themselves, and it serves as a strong counter-argument to the idea that AI will simply centralize all value within a few model labs.
> A: 是的，最具吸引力的增长领域之一是 AEO，即 “回答引擎优化”。正如 SEO 出现以帮助公司在 Google 搜索结果中显现，AEO 是公司用来跟踪自身在 AI 模型中的表现以及确定是否被推荐的软件。这是一个新的、快速增长的领域，不是由模型公司主导，并且它强有力地反驳了人工智能将简单地将所有价值集中在少数模型实验室中的观点。

**Q: How is AI affecting the relationship between company headcount and revenue growth?**
> **Q: 人工智能如何影响公司员工人数与收入增长之间的关系？**

A: We are starting to see a decoupling of revenue growth from headcount growth, particularly at software companies. There is a less obvious connection now between growing your team and growing your revenue. However, it is important to note that many firms adopting AI are also high-growth companies with significant opportunities ahead of them, so it is difficult to isolate whether these changes are solely due to AI or simply the nature of those firms.
> A: 我们开始看到收入增长与员工人数增长的脱钩，特别是在软件公司中。现在，扩大团队与增加收入之间的联系不再那么明显。然而，需要注意的是，许多采用 AI 的公司也是具有重大机会的高增长公司，因此很难孤立出这些变化是否完全由于 AI，或仅仅是这些公司的性质造成的。

**Q: Why are some legacy companies more hesitant to implement AI than others?**
> **Q: 为什么有些传统公司比其他公司更不愿意实施 AI？**

A: Not every firm views AI as a transformative necessity that requires immediate, aggressive implementation. For example, we’ve seen accounting firms take vastly different positions; some are rushing to implement agents, while others, like Deloitte, have taken a more hedged, conservative approach, positioning themselves as using AI to augment rather than replace workers. This shows that the response to AI is not uniform—it is a strategic choice based on a firm's specific risk tolerance and business model.
> A: 并不是每家公司都将人工智能视为一种需要立即积极实施的变革性必要性。例如，我们看到会计公司采取截然不同的立场；一些公司急于实施代理，而其他公司如德勤则采取了更加保守的态度，定位于使用 AI 来增强而非替代工人。这表明对人工智能的反应并不统一——这是基于公司特定风险承受能力和商业模式的战略选择。

**Q: What is the reality behind the claim that a majority of startups are using DeepSeek because it is cheap?**
> **Q: 大多数初创公司使用 DeepSeek 是因为它便宜的说法背后有何现实？**

A: That claim is largely inaccurate. While we did see a spike in adoption for DeepSeek when it first launched, it never hit even 1% of the firms on our platform. DeepSeek faces significant hurdles, particularly regarding security perceptions; businesses are often wary of using it, even if they are locally hosting it. I don't see DeepSeek gaining widespread adoption quickly because there are now many other cheaper or open-source alternatives available that don't carry the same baggage.
> A: 这个说法在很大程度上是不准确的。虽然在 DeepSeek 首次推出时我们确实看到采用量的激增，但它从未在我们平台上的公司中占到 1%。DeepSeek 面临着重大障碍，特别是在安全感知方面；即使是本地托管，企业通常也会对使用它持谨慎态度。我认为 DeepSeek 不会迅速获得广泛的采用，因为现在有许多其他更便宜或者开源的替代品可用，并且这些替代品并不存在相同的负担。

---

**Q: Can AI improve the quality of journalism, or is it just a tool for summary?**
> **Q: AI 能否提高新闻报道的质量，还是仅仅是一个摘要工具？**

A: AI is quite bad at opinionated or deep, creative writing, but it is very good at highly structured tasks, such as summarizing meetings or extracting bullet points from complex information. In journalism, AI will never replace the investigative work of going on the ground, interviewing sources, and securing sealed documents. However, there is a world where reporters use AI to handle the straight summary of their findings, allowing them to focus more time on the actual, human-centric reporting work.
> A: 人工智能在带有观点或深度创意写作方面很糟糕，但它在高度结构化的任务方面表现得很好，例如总结会议或从复杂信息中提取要点。在新闻报道中，人工智能永远无法替代实地调查的工作，包括采访来源和获取密件。然而，有一种场景是记者使用人工智能来处理他们发现的直接摘要，从而让他们可以将更多的时间集中于真正以人为本的报道工作。

---

**Q: What is the "bull case" for companies like Cursor in the current AI market?**
> **Q: 在当前的人工智能市场中，Cursor 等公司的 “牛市案例” 是什么？**

A: The bull case for Cursor is its ability to provide a superior developer experience, specifically through intelligent model routing. While model providers like OpenAI and Anthropic are incentivized to keep users on their own models to maximize token revenue, a tool like Cursor can automatically route tasks to the most efficient or cost-effective model. This creates a competitive pressure that forces the model companies to improve their own product experiences, as they can no longer rely solely on the "frontier" status of their models.
> A: Cursor 的牛市案例在于其提供了卓越的开发者体验，特别是通过智能模型路由。虽然像 OpenAI 和 Anthropic 这样的模型提供商被激励保持用户在自己的模型中以最大化代币收入，但像 Cursor 这样的工具可以自动将任务路由到最有效或最具成本效益的模型。这产生了竞争压力，迫使模型公司改善自身的产品体验，因为它们不能再单靠其模型的 “前沿” 地位来吸引用户。
