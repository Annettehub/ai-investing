# Summary

AI infrastructure is undergoing a fundamental transformation as existing systems are revamped to support large-scale AI workloads, including new requirements for vector storage, orchestration, and agent-based tooling. Jennifer Li, a general partner at Andreessen Horowitz, highlights that in this "distribution era," the speed at which an AI-native company establishes itself as the default brand is the primary determinant of long-term success. ElevenLabs serves as a benchmark for this strategy, effectively bridging the gap between developer-focused APIs and enterprise-ready creative suites. While frontier models continue to accelerate, the rapid advancement of open-source models and vision-language capabilities is creating new opportunities for complex, multi-modal workflows. Ultimately, AI tools act as powerful force multipliers for human creativity, enabling smaller teams to produce professional-grade content and democratizing access to sophisticated storytelling technologies.
> AI 基础设施正在经历根本性的转变，因为现有系统正在重新设计以支持大规模的 AI 工作负载，包括对向量存储、编排和基于代理的工具的新需求。安德森·霍洛维茨的合伙人詹妮弗·李强调，在这个 “分发时代”，一个 AI 原生公司确立自身为默认品牌的速度是决定其长期成功的主要因素。ElevenLabs 作为这一策略的基准，有效地弥补了面向开发者的 API 与企业级创意套件之间的差距。尽管前沿模型仍在加速发展，开源模型和视觉 - 语言能力的快速进步正在为复杂的多模态工作流程创造新的机会。最终，AI 工具成为人类创造力的强大助推器，使得较小的团队能够制作专业级内容，并使得复杂的叙事技术变得人人可及。

# Takeaways

* Speed to becoming the default brand is the primary competitive moat in the AI-native era, creating a winner-take-most dynamic that differentiates it from traditional enterprise software.
  > 在 AI 原生时代，成为默认品牌的速度是主要的竞争护城河，创造了一种赢家通吃的大动态，使其与传统企业软件区别开来。
* Unlike the speculative infrastructure overbuild of the late 1990s, 90% of current AI infrastructure capacity is pre-committed, signaling a demand-driven rather than supply-driven market expansion.
  > 与 1990 年代末的投机性基础设施过度建设不同，目前 90% 的 AI 基础设施容量是预先承诺的，这表明市场扩张是需求驱动而不是供应驱动的。
* The software development lifecycle has shifted to an agent-first model, with over 90% of code now generated by AI, requiring a complete redesign of traditional developer tooling and security protocols.
  > 软件开发生命周期已经转向以代理为先的模型，现在超过 90% 的代码由 AI 生成，要求对传统开发工具和安全协议进行全面重新设计。
* Elite AI founders maintain a competitive advantage by building "patchwork" product features that anticipate future model capabilities, allowing them to deliver value before the underlying technology is fully optimized.
  > 精英 AI 创始人通过构建 “拼凑” 产品特性来保持竞争优势，这些特性预见了未来模型的能力，使他们能够在基础技术完全优化之前提供价值。
* Creative and media AI applications thrive because they operate outside the strict accuracy requirements of traditional software, where system imperfections are often perceived as artistic variations rather than functional errors.
  > 创意和媒体 AI 应用得以蓬勃发展，因为它们运作在传统软件严格的准确性要求之外，系统的不完美通常被视为艺术变体，而非功能错误。
* AI infrastructure is evolving through a layered integration model, where new components like vector storage and orchestration layers are stacked upon existing transactional databases rather than replacing them entirely.
  > AI 基础设施正通过分层集成模型发展，新组件如向量存储和编排层在现有事务数据库之上堆叠，而不是完全替代它们。
* Voice agents represent a high-velocity use case because they effectively automate repetitive, natural language-based workflows without the need for specialized technical jargon.
  > 语音代理代表了一个快速发展的用例，因为它们有效地自动化了重复的、基于自然语言的工作流程，而无需专业的技术术语。
* Legacy B2B SaaS companies can achieve significant re-acceleration by pivoting to AI-native products, effectively using new technology to revitalize long-standing, traditional software offerings.
  > 传统的 B2B SaaS 公司可以通过转向 AI 原生产品来实现显著的重新加速，有效地利用新技术来振兴长期存在的传统软件产品。

# Q & A

**Q: Why is there such a massive, dedicated focus on AI infrastructure right now?**
> **Q: 为什么现在对人工智能基础设施有如此大的专注？**

A: We are currently in a phase where the existing infrastructure—originally built for cloud computing—is not optimized for the specific demands of AI workloads. Whether it is compute, storage, or the orchestration layers for agents, everything is being revamped in real-time. We raised $1.7 billion specifically for this because we are in the early innings of developing the research and algorithms that will power future models, and supporting the founders building this foundational stack is essential for the next generation of software.
> A: 我们目前处于一个阶段，现有的基础设施——最初为云计算构建——并未针对人工智能工作负载的特定需求进行优化。无论是计算、存储，还是代理的编排层，一切都在实时更新。我们专门筹集了 17 亿美元用于此，因为我们正处在开发将推动未来模型的研究和算法的早期阶段，支持构建这一基础堆栈的创始人对下一代软件至关重要。

**Q: How does the current AI landscape differ from the internet bubble of the late 90s?**
> **Q: 当前的人工智能景观与 90 年代末的互联网泡沫有何不同？**

A: While the high-level growth metrics might rhyme with 1998, the core economic drivers are fundamentally different. First, the scale is vastly larger: the internet had 70 million users four years after launch, whereas AI apps have already reached one billion monthly active users. Second, 90% of the current AI infrastructure build-out is pre-committed, compared to only 3% during the fiber-optic build-out of the early 2000s. We are also facing physical constraints—energy, land, and GPU supply—that make this build-out a much more deliberate, demand-driven process than the over-capacity era of the past.
> A: 虽然高层次的增长指标可能与 1998 年有相似之处，但核心经济驱动因素有根本性的不同。首先，规模大得多：互联网在推出四年后有 7000 万用户，而人工智能应用已经达到每月 10 亿活跃用户。其次，目前人工智能基础设施建设的 90% 是预先承诺的，而在 2000 年代初光纤建设时只有 3%。我们还面临物理限制——能源、土地和 GPU 供应——使得这次建设比过去的过剩时代更加慎重和需求导向。

**Q: Why is distribution becoming the primary differentiator for AI-native companies?**
> **Q: 为什么分销成为人工智能原生公司的主要差异化因素？**

A: In the AI-native era, we have entered the "distribution era." Because new categories and products are being created daily, the speed at which a company can become the "default brand" is the most critical factor for long-term success. Once a product establishes itself as the go-to solution—like Harvey in the legal space or ElevenLabs in voice—the gap between the leader and the rest of the field widens rapidly. You need to build a flywheel and establish that brand recognition from day zero to survive in such a crowded market.
> A: 在人工智能原生时代，我们进入了 “分销时代”。因为新的类别和产品每天都在创造，一个公司成为 “默认品牌” 的速度是长期成功的最关键因素。一旦一个产品确立了作为首选解决方案的地位——比如法律领域的 Harvey 或语音领域的 ElevenLabs——领先者与其他竞争者之间的差距会迅速扩大。你需要建立一个飞轮，并从零开始确立那个品牌认知，以便在如此拥挤的市场中生存。

**Q: What specific traits do you look for when sizing up founders in the AI space?**
> **Q: 在评估人工智能领域的创始人时，你会寻找什么特定特征？**

A: Beyond raw technical talent, the best founders possess deep conviction and a personal connection to the problem they are solving. They are also highly product-minded. Many technical founders fall into the trap of thinking "the best tech will win," but the reality is that the tech is only 60% of the equation. The best founders know how to package complex AI models into products that are easy for users to consume, often using product design to bridge the gap between what a model can do today and what the user actually needs.
> A: 除了原始的技术才能外，最佳的创始人具备深厚的信念和与他们所解决的问题的个人联系。他们也非常关注产品。许多技术创始人陷入 “最好的技术将胜出” 的误区，但现实是，技术仅占方程式的 60%。最佳的创始人知道如何将复杂的人工智能模型打包成用户易于使用的产品，往往利用产品设计弥补模型当前能力与用户实际需求之间的差距。

**Q: How should founders handle the gap between current model capabilities and their desired product roadmap?**
> **Q: 创始人应该如何处理当前模型能力与他们期望的产品路线图之间的差距？**

A: The most successful founders understand where the model capabilities are heading and build their product roadmap in concert with that trajectory. If a feature is highly requested but the model isn't quite ready to support it perfectly, they will build a "patchwork" version of that functionality to get it into customers' hands immediately. Then, as model capabilities improve, they backfill that functionality with the actual model. This allows them to stay ahead of the market while delivering value today.
> A: 最成功的创始人理解模型能力的方向，并与该轨迹一致地建立产品路线图。如果某个功能非常被请求，但模型尚未完全支持，他们会构建一个 “拼接版” 的功能，立即将其交给客户使用。然后，随着模型能力的提高，他们会用实际模型来填补该功能。这使他们能够在市场上保持领先，同时今天交付价值。

**Q: Will AI replace human creativity in storytelling and media?**
> **Q: 人工智能会取代人类在叙事和媒体创作中的创造力吗？**

A: I don't believe AI will replace directors, authors, or anyone who has a clear vision in their mind. Creativity is inherently a form of human expression and intent. Instead, AI acts as a powerful tool that democratizes access to creation. It allows a one-to-two-person studio to produce content that previously required tens of people and months of work. The goal is for creatives to embrace these tools to become more productive and expressive, allowing them to bring their ideas to life faster and reach a broader audience.
> A: 我认为人工智能不会取代导演、作者或任何在脑海中有明确愿景的人。创造力本质上是一种人类表达和意图的形式。相反，人工智能作为一种强大的工具，民主化了创作的机会。它使得一到两人的工作室能够制作以前需要数十人和数月工作的内容。目标是让创作者拥抱这些工具，提高生产力和表现力，使他们能够更快地将想法变为现实并接触到更广泛的受众。

The key is that the AI product doesn't just replace the old software; it re-accelerates the core product. By integrating AI as a core component of the workflow, companies can find new life and growth in their traditional offerings that would have otherwise stagnated.

> 人工智能基础设施的战略重组

ElevenLabs serves as a primary example of this evolution, moving from simple synthetic voice narration to powering complex voice agents in customer support and enterprise workflows. The focus remains on supporting foundation model research, developer tooling for AI-generated code, and security frameworks that protect both software and organizational parameters.
